# Reading a 3rd Gear Pull

> **Source confidence:** High confidence (method). Specific values — verify by VIN/ROM ID.

## What it is

A "3rd gear pull" is the standard way to datalog the N54. You take a steady wide-open-throttle pull in a higher gear (commonly 3rd) so the engine spends enough **time under load** to show how boost, fueling, ignition, and load behave. A higher gear loads the engine longer than 1st/2nd, making the data clearer and the pull more comparable run to run.

## Why it matters

A consistent, repeatable pull is what makes a log meaningful. Same gear, warmed-up car, similar conditions → you can compare pulls and trust the trends. Random part-throttle driving cannot answer "is boost holding target" or "is it knocking."

## Before you pull (prerequisites)

- Car **warmed up** (coolant and oil at temp).
- **No active faults**; maintenance current (plugs, coils, fuel system, vacuum lines).
- A **safe, legal** place — a private setting such as a dyno is ideal. This is an educational diagnostic method, not a guide to do anything illegal on a public road.
- Logging tool set up with the right channels (below).

## What to log

At minimum:

- [Boost target vs actual](/wiki/logging/boost-target-vs-actual) + [WGDC](/wiki/logging/wgdc)
- [Timing corrections](/wiki/logging/timing-corrections) (per cylinder)
- [Lambda / AFR](/wiki/logging/lambda-afr)
- [HPFP / rail pressure](/wiki/logging/hpfp-pressure) (+ [LPFP](/wiki/logging/lpfp-pressure) on built fuel systems)
- [Throttle angle](/wiki/logging/throttle-angle) + pedal
- [Load requested vs actual](/wiki/logging/load-requested-vs-actual)
- [IAT](/wiki/logging/iat), coolant/oil temp, gear, RPM

## How to read it — what a good pull looks like

- **Boost:** actual tracks target; WGDC not pinned.
- **Timing:** little/no pull; cylinders similar.
- **Fueling:** lambda at target; rail pressure holds target to the top.
- **Throttle:** stays open while pedal is flat (no closures).
- **Load:** actual rises with requested.
- **Temps:** IAT and coolant in a reasonable range; not climbing hard pull to pull.

## How to read it — what a bad pull looks like

- Boost short with **WGDC maxed** → leak/wastegate (see [Low Boost](/wiki/diagnostics/low-boost), [30FF](/wiki/diagnostics/30ff-underboost)).
- **Negative timing** under load → knock (see [Timing Corrections](/wiki/diagnostics/timing-corrections)).
- **Rail pressure crashing + lean lambda** → fuel-supply limit (see [Fuel Pressure Crash](/wiki/diagnostics/fuel-pressure-crash)).
- **Throttle closing** with pedal flat → intervention/limiter (see [Throttle Closures](/wiki/diagnostics/throttle-closures)).
- IAT climbing each run → heat soak; let it cool.

## When to stop

Stop the pull immediately if you see knock (sustained timing pull), a rail-pressure crash with lean lambda, overboost, or rising coolant/oil temps. These are [unsafe-log](/wiki/logging/unsafe-logs) conditions — fix the hardware/fuel/ignition cause before pulling again. **Hardware and fueling get fixed first; tuning comes after the baseline is healthy.**

## Common interpretation mistakes

- Comparing pulls from **different gears / temps / states of tune**.
- Logging too few channels to see context.
- Reacting to one noisy sample instead of a repeatable pattern.
- Treating a mechanical fault (leak, worn pump, bad coil) as a tuning value to adjust.
