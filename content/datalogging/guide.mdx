# Datalogging Guide

Datalogging is how you verify that your car is running correctly. On a modified N54, it is the difference between catching a problem early and paying for an engine rebuild.

**Log every WOT (wide-open throttle) pull. Review logs before adding more power. This is not optional.**

---

## Why Datalogging Matters

The N54's ECU has a knock correction system that retards timing when it detects borderline detonation. This protects the engine in the short term but is not a substitute for proper calibration. Persistent knock corrections mean:

- Your tune is too aggressive for your current fuel or hardware
- Your hardware has degraded (worn plugs, failing HPFP, heat soak)
- You need to revisit the calibration

Without logs, you are guessing. With logs, you have evidence.

---

## Logging Software Options

### MHD Flasher (iOS / Android)
MHD is the primary flash tuning and logging tool for the N54. It connects via Bluetooth OBD2 adapter (BIMMERCODE/OBD2 BT4.0 compatible) and logs directly from the ECU.

- Best-in-class channel depth for N54
- Overlay on phone screen while driving
- Exportable logs for tuner review

### BimmerLink
BimmerLink can log many of the same channels without requiring a flash. Useful for diagnosing stock or lightly modified cars but lacks some N54-specific channels available via MHD.

### INPA / ISTA
OEM BMW diagnostic software provides live data but is less practical for driving logs. Used more for diagnostic inspection than road data capture.

---

## Required OBD2 Adapter

MHD requires a **compatible Bluetooth 4.0 OBD2 adapter**. Not all cheap adapters work reliably. Recommended:

- **BIMMERCODE/MHD-recommended BT adapters** — verified to work without dropout
- Avoid ELM327 clones — they often lose connection under load

The OBD2 port is under the dashboard, driver's side.

---

## Setting Up MHD Logging

1. Open MHD → **Logs** tab
2. Select **New Log Profile** or use an existing N54 profile
3. Add channels (see [MHD Log Channel Guide](/datalogging/mhd-channels) for essential channels)
4. Start the car, connect the adapter
5. Drive normally to operating temperature before logging WOT pulls

---

## Logging Procedure

### Before every WOT pull:
- Car at full operating temperature (oil temp, coolant temp at normal range)
- Transmission in appropriate gear (3rd gear on manual, or consistent gear for automatic)
- Roll-on from ~2,000 RPM to redline (or desired pull range)
- Do not shift during a pull if possible — one uninterrupted log segment is most readable

### How many pulls:
- On a fresh tune: minimum 3–5 WOT pulls from the same conditions (same gear, similar speed)
- Reviewers need repeated pulls to distinguish one-off anomalies from consistent patterns

### When to stop and review:
- Any timing correction more negative than approximately –2° to –3° on a consistent basis
- Fuel pressure drop under load
- Boost spike followed by sharp cut
- IAT above ~55–60°C (check with your tuner for their threshold)

## Sending Logs to Your Tuner

MHD saves logs in `.csv` format. Export and send them to your tuner via email or their submission system. Include:

- **Fuel being used** (octane, blend percentage if ethanol)
- **Weather conditions** (hot day? Cold start?)
- **Any symptoms** you noticed during or after the pull
- **Hardware changes** since the last log session

## How Often Should You Log?

| Situation | Frequency |
|---|---|
| Fresh tune | Every WOT pull for first 2 weeks |
| New hardware added | Every WOT pull until confirmed good |
| Fuel batch changed | First pull on new fuel |
| Normal maintenance interval | At least monthly on a regularly driven tuned car |
| Before any power increase | Confirm current stage is clean first |
