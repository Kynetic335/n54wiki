# Coolant Temps

> **Source confidence:** Needs validation

## What it is

Coolant temperature is a primary health signal. The DME uses it for fueling, protection, and to trigger limp mode if it climbs too high.

## Why it matters

Rising coolant temps under load can cause timing pull and power cuts; it is a key log channel.

## How it works

A sensor reports coolant temp; the DME manages cooling and protection around it.

## Common symptoms or failure modes

- Climbing coolant temp under load
- Overheat warnings

## What to check in logs

- Coolant temp

## Mechanical checks first

Confirm cooling system health before attributing power loss to the tune.
