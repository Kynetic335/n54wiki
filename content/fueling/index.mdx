# Fuel Grades: 91 / 93 / E30 / E50 / E85

Fuel is the foundation of your tune. The N54's ability to make power safely is directly tied to fuel quality and octane. Understanding your fuel options is essential before choosing a tune map or ethanol blend.

---

## Octane and Why It Matters

**Octane rating** measures a fuel's resistance to autoignition (knock). Higher octane allows the ECU to advance ignition timing further and maintain higher cylinder pressure before detonation occurs.

On the N54:
- More timing = more power and efficiency
- Detonation (knock) = rapid engine damage
- The tune is calibrated for a specific fuel; running lower octane than the tune expects will cause the knock correction system to retard timing

---

## Pump Gas: 91 vs 93 Octane

### 91 Octane (most of western US, some regions)
91 octane is the minimum practical fuel for a tuned N54. The ECU has enough headroom to run safely, but timing targets are more conservative. Expect modestly lower power than a 93-octane tune would produce.

### 93 Octane (most of eastern US, Canada)
The N54 tuning baseline. The majority of MHD base maps and custom tunes target 93 octane. More timing headroom means more power and better efficiency.

### 91 vs 93 in Practice
If you live in a 91-octane region, your tuner should calibrate for 91 as the base. Running a 93 tune on 91 octane will cause timing corrections — the ECU will compensate, but you're pushing a tune designed for fuel you don't have.

## Ethanol Blends

Ethanol (ethyl alcohol) has two key properties that make it attractive for performance tuning:

1. **High octane equivalency** — ethanol's research octane number (RON) is approximately 109; pump E85 blends vary but consistently outperform premium pump gas in octane resistance
2. **High latent heat of vaporization** — ethanol absorbs significantly more heat as it evaporates in the intake tract, cooling the charge air and reducing knock tendency

### E30 (30% ethanol, 70% gasoline)
E30 is the most popular ethanol strategy for N54 daily drivers. It offers:
- Meaningful octane improvement over 93 pump gas
- Charge cooling benefit
- Compatible with stock HPFP and most Stage 1–2 builds
- Requires a specific E30 tune (not a 93-octane map)

### E50 (50% ethanol)
More aggressive than E30. Requires:
- LPFP upgrade on most N54 builds (stock LPFP struggles to supply sufficient volume at E50+)
- Confirmed HPFP health
- A custom tune calibrated for E50

### E85 (approximately 70–85% ethanol — pump E85 varies)
E85 pump fuel varies in ethanol content seasonally and regionally (typically 51–83%). For precise tuning:
- A flex-fuel sensor is ideal to measure actual content
- All fueling hardware must be rated for high ethanol (check injectors, lines, and pump compatibility)
- High fuel flow demand: LPFP upgrade required, port injection may be necessary on higher power builds

---

## Mixing Your Own Blend

Many N54 owners mix their own ethanol blends by adding E85 to premium pump gas. Use the **[E30 / E50 Blend Calculator](/fueling/blend-calculator)** to get the exact number of gallons to add based on your current fuel level and E85 source — no guessing.

- **Always shake a sample after mixing** to confirm even mixture
- **Measure with a flex-fuel tester or ethanol content tester** before the first WOT pull on a new batch
- **Fill the tank at least halfway** before adding ethanol to ensure proper mixing

## Tune Compatibility Summary

| Fuel | Requires specific tune? | Hardware changes? |
|---|---|---|
| 91 octane | Yes — calibrated for 91 | None |
| 93 octane | Yes — standard baseline | None |
| E30 | Yes | None on most builds |
| E50 | Yes | LPFP upgrade |
| E85 | Yes — flex or E85-specific | LPFP + possible port injection |

---

## Fuel and Knock

Regardless of fuel, you must **monitor timing corrections in your datalogs**. If the ECU is pulling timing on what should be a well-calibrated blend, you may have:
- Lower octane than expected (bad E85 batch, stale ethanol content)
- Carbon deposits causing hot spots
- A tune that needs revision

See [Timing Corrections / Knock](/datalogging/timing-knock) for log interpretation guidance.
