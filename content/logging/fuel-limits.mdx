# Logging Fuel Limits

> **Source confidence:** Needs validation

## What it is

Fuel limits appear in logs when the system cannot deliver the fuel demanded — rail pressure dropping below target, or mixture going lean under load.

## Why it matters

Hitting a fuel limit is dangerous (lean under boost) and caps power; it is a stop-and-fix condition.

## How it works

When pumps/injectors max out, rail pressure falls and mixture leans relative to target.

## Common symptoms or failure modes

- Rail pressure below target
- Lean under load
- LPFP pressure dropping

## What to check in logs

- Fuel rail pressure target vs actual
- LPFP pressure
- Lambda/AFR

## Mechanical checks first

Address pump/injector capacity; do not push past a fuel limit.
