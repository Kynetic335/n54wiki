# Long Crank

> **Source confidence:** Needs validation

## What it is

Long crank is when the engine takes longer than normal to start, frequently linked to fuel pressure (HPFP/LPFP) on the N54.

## Why it matters

It is a classic early sign of a failing HPFP, though other causes exist.

## How it works

If fuel pressure is not built quickly at start, cranking is extended.

## Common symptoms or failure modes

- Extended cranking before start
- Hard starts when cold/hot

## What to check in logs

- Fuel rail pressure at start
- LPFP pressure

## Mechanical checks first

Check HPFP/LPFP and rail pressure; this is usually mechanical.

## Related codes or fault patterns

- Fuel pressure faults (e.g. 29DC) — verify by scan.
