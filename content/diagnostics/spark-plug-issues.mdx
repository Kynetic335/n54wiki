# Spark Plug Issues

> **Source confidence:** High confidence (behavior). Gap/heat-range specs — verify by VIN/ROM ID.

## What the symptom means

Spark plugs ignite the mixture. As they wear, the gap opens and the spark gets harder to maintain — and higher cylinder pressure under boost makes that worse. The classic result is a misfire that appears or worsens **under load**. Tuned N54s commonly run a colder heat range and a **smaller gap** to keep spark stable; the exact spec depends on the build. **Verify gap/heat range by source.**

## Common causes

- **Worn plugs** (gap opened over time).
- **Wrong gap** for the boost level (too large → spark blowout under load).
- **Wrong heat range** for the application.
- Plugs stressed by a **weak coil** (check coils too).

## What to log

- Misfire counters **per cylinder**
- [Timing corrections](/wiki/logging/timing-corrections)
- RPM, gear, load

## What a healthy pattern looks like

- Misfire counters near zero through the pull.
- No cylinder standing out under boost.

## What a bad pattern can suggest

- **Misfire that appears/worsens as boost climbs** → spark blowout from worn/over-gapped plugs.
- Several cylinders misfiring under boost after a while since last service → plugs due.
- One cylinder only → could be that plug or that coil (swap-test to separate).

## Mechanical checks first

1. **Inspect plugs** for wear/condition.
2. **Verify gap** to the spec for the build (Source needed for exact value).
3. Confirm correct **heat range**.
4. **Swap-test coils** to separate plug vs coil.

## Tuning-related causes second

Plug condition is maintenance, not a tuning value. Do not pull timing in the calibration to stop a misfire caused by worn or over-gapped plugs — replace/re-gap the plugs.

## When to stop tuning

Stop if misfires appear under boost. Service the plugs (correct gap/heat range) before further tuning.
