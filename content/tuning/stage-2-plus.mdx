# Stage 2+

Stage 2+ represents the upper end of what the stock N54 turbos can produce. This tier typically adds **methanol injection (meth/water-methanol injection, WMI)** or uses **higher ethanol blends (E40–E50)** alongside the Stage 2 hardware package to squeeze additional power without stepping up to hybrid turbos.

---

## What Stage 2+ Adds Over Stage 2

| Addition | Benefit |
|---|---|
| Methanol injection | Dramatically lowers IAT; adds octane equivalency; allows more timing and boost |
| Higher ethanol content (E40–E50) | More fuel octane headroom; cooler charge temps via ethanol's high latent heat |
| Aggressive tune calibration | Higher boost targets, more timing, revised fueling — requires review of every log pull |

---

## Methanol Injection (WMI)

### How it works
A methanol/water mixture is sprayed into the intake tract (typically post-FMIC). The liquid absorbs heat as it evaporates, lowering charge air temperature. Methanol also acts as an octane improver, giving the ECU more room for timing advance.

### Common systems
- **Snow Performance** (Stage 2+, Boost Cooler kits)
- **AEM Water-Methanol Injection**
- **Aquamist** (more precise, adjustable)

### What to log with meth
You must log IAT with and without methanol to confirm the system is functioning. A meth system that's low on fluid, clogged, or malfunctioning reverts your tune to running conditions it wasn't calibrated for — a lean, hot, high-boost condition that causes knock.

**Always run a failsafe:** configure your meth controller to pull boost or trigger a safe mode if the nozzle fails to spray.

---

## Ethanol at Stage 2+

Running E40–E50 at Stage 2+ hardware levels is a common and effective strategy. Ethanol's high octane rating and high latent heat of vaporization give the ECU more headroom for:

- More ignition timing advance
- Higher boost targets
- Richer WOT calibration for cooler combustion

### Fueling system requirements at E40–E50
At E40+, the stock LPFP begins to limit fuel delivery on some builds. An upgraded in-tank pump (AEM 320, Walbro 450, or a port injection kit) is recommended before running E50 or higher.

See [HPFP / LPFP / Port Injection](/fueling/hpfp-lpfp-port-injection) for fuel pump upgrade guidance.

## What the Stock Turbos Can Realistically Support

The stock IHI twin turbos have limits. General community understanding:

- Beyond a certain boost level (varies by turbo condition and back pressure), the stock turbos lose efficiency and exhaust gas temperatures (EGT) climb
- Spinning the stock turbos past their efficiency island increases heat rather than power
- The stock turbos are generally considered to top out somewhere in the neighborhood of their peak flow capacity — running them beyond this point is where you see diminishing returns and increased risk

> **Note:** Exact limits depend on your turbo condition, ambient temp, fuel, and tune calibration. Stock turbos that have seen 80,000–100,000 miles may have degraded seals and wheel condition.

---

## Stage 2+ Checklist

Before going Stage 2+:

- [ ] FMIC installed and charge pipes aluminum
- [ ] HPFP confirmed healthy under load (log it)
- [ ] Spark plugs fresh, iridium, gapped tightly
- [ ] LPFP upgraded if running E40+
- [ ] Methanol system installed, tested, and failsafe configured
- [ ] Boost solenoids fresh
- [ ] Tuner is aware of all hardware and fuel changes
