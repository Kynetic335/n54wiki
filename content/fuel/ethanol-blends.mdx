# Ethanol Blends

> **Source confidence:** Needs validation

## What it is

Ethanol blends (such as E30, E50, E85) mix gasoline with ethanol. Higher ethanol content changes the fuel's knock resistance and the volume of fuel required.

## Why it matters

Ethanol raises effective knock resistance but demands more fuel volume, pushing harder on the LPFP, HPFP, and injectors. Blend must match the calibration assumptions.

## How it works

Higher ethanol needs more fuel mass for the same air, so fuel-system demand rises. A blend mismatch versus the tune causes lean or rich conditions.

## Common symptoms or failure modes

- Lean condition if blend is higher than the tune assumes
- Fuel system running out of capacity on high blends

## What to check in logs

- Fuel trims
- Rail pressure target vs actual
- Timing corrections (knock context)

## Mechanical checks first

Confirm actual blend and fuel-system capacity before relying on a high-ethanol calibration.
