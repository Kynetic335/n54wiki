# Torque Limiters

> **Source confidence:** Needs validation

> Educational reference only. This page does not publish tune values.

## What this map controls

Torque limiter maps cap the torque the DME will request or deliver, for protection and drivability (including drivetrain protection).

## Why it matters

Limiters can hold power below hardware capability and cause throttle closures. Changing them affects protection and drivetrain stress.

## Related log channels

- Torque request
- Requested vs actual load
- Throttle angle

## What changes are visible in logs

A limiter shows as a flat torque/load ceiling and possible throttle closures.

## What can go wrong if changed blindly

- Removing drivetrain protection
- Throttle closures if conflicting
- Stress on transmission/clutch

## Mechanical problems that can look like a tuning problem

- Boost leaks (look like a shortfall, not a cap)
