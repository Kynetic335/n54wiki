# What Load Means

> **Source confidence:** Needs validation

## What it is

Engine load is the DME's measure of how much air (and thus torque potential) the engine is taking in, often expressed as a percentage relative to a reference. It is central to the N54 control model.

## Why it matters

Boost, fueling, and timing are scheduled against load. Reading requested vs actual load tells you whether the engine is meeting its target.

## How it works

The DME estimates load from airflow/manifold pressure and uses it as the main axis for many maps.

## Common symptoms or failure modes

- Actual load falling short of requested (a shortfall to diagnose)

## What to check in logs

- Requested load
- Actual load

## Mechanical checks first

Load shortfalls can be mechanical (boost leak) or limiter-driven; check both.
