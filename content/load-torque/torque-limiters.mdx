# Torque Limiters

> **Source confidence:** Needs validation

## What it is

Torque limiters are calibration limits that cap the torque the DME will request or deliver, for protection and drivability.

## Why it matters

A limiter can hold power below what the hardware could make, showing up as a flat ceiling in logs.

## How it works

The torque request is clamped to the limiter value; downstream load/boost follow the clamp.

## Common symptoms or failure modes

- Flat torque/load ceiling regardless of more pedal/boost

## What to check in logs

- Torque request
- Requested vs actual load

## Mechanical checks first

A flat ceiling that is not a leak usually means a limiter.
