# MHD Channels

> **Source confidence:** High confidence (concept). Exact channel names/units vary by tool and DME — verify by VIN/ROM ID.

## What the channels mean

A datalogging tool reads values from the DME in real time and records them over a pull so you can review them afterward. "Channels" are those individual values — boost, fuel pressure, timing corrections, temperatures, and so on. MHD is one common N54 tool; the concepts here apply to any logger. Exact channel names, units, and availability depend on the tool and the DME/ROM. **Verify by VIN/ROM ID.**

## Why it matters

You cannot diagnose what you do not log. Picking the right channels — and enough of them to give context — is what turns a vague "it feels flat" into "boost is 4 psi under target with WGDC maxed, so it's a leak." Logging the wrong or too few channels leads to guessing.

## Core channels and what each answers

| Channel | Answers |
|---|---|
| [Boost target vs actual](/wiki/logging/boost-target-vs-actual) | Is boost control healthy? Under/overboost? |
| [WGDC](/wiki/logging/wgdc) | Is the controller working or fighting a leak? |
| [Timing corrections](/wiki/logging/timing-corrections) | Is the engine knocking? |
| [Lambda / AFR](/wiki/logging/lambda-afr) | Is the mixture safe (lean/rich)? |
| [HPFP / rail pressure](/wiki/logging/hpfp-pressure) | Is fuel supply holding under load? |
| [LPFP pressure](/wiki/logging/lpfp-pressure) | Is the low-side pump the limit? |
| [Throttle angle](/wiki/logging/throttle-angle) | Torque cuts / closures? |
| [Load requested vs actual](/wiki/logging/load-requested-vs-actual) | Is the engine meeting its load target? |
| [IAT](/wiki/logging/iat) | Heat soak / knock risk from intake temp |
| Coolant / oil temp | Cooling health, when to stop |
| Gear / RPM | Context to make pulls comparable |

## What normal behavior generally looks like

- Channels move together in ways that make physical sense (boost up, load up, throttle open).
- No channel pinned at a limit while the result it controls falls short.
- Repeatable pull to pull on a warmed-up car.

## What abnormal behavior can suggest

- A control output pinned (e.g. WGDC maxed) with the controlled value short → mechanical limit.
- Safety channels reacting (timing pull, lean lambda, rail-pressure drop) → stop and diagnose.
- One channel implausible vs the others → possible sensor fault or wrong scaling.

## Which diagnostics use these channels

Essentially all of them — see [Diagnostics](/wiki/diagnostics). Each diagnostic page lists the specific channels that prove or disprove that issue.

## Common interpretation mistakes

- Logging too few channels to see context (e.g. boost without WGDC).
- Comparing pulls taken in different gears, temps, or states of tune.
- Trusting a channel from a faulty sensor or wrong sensor scaling.
- Reacting to a single noisy sample instead of a repeatable trend.
