# N54 Tuning School Content Rules

Write for a public educational wiki. Keep content practical, technical, and safe.

## Required Boundaries

- Present approved educational calibration information only.
- Do not expose raw private BIN, XDF, DAMOS, A2L, OLS, customer tune, or customer log files.
- Do not add flashing, BIN writing, checksum correction, tune generation, or binary patching workflows.
- Do not publish exact private table addresses, raw definitions, proprietary map dumps, or customer-specific values.
- Do not provide unsafe copy-paste tune values.

## Content Shape

- Start with the concept, then explain why it matters on the N54 platform.
- Separate prerequisites, assumptions, procedure, validation, and risks.
- Prefer public-facing explanations over internal implementation detail.
- Include source confidence labels for technical claims.
- State when something requires datalog validation or professional calibration review.

## Safety Notes

Call out safety concerns when relevant: fuel quality, boost control, knock, lambda/AFR, injector headroom, HPFP/LPFP limits, IAT, EGT, coolant/oil temperature, torque/load limits, emissions compliance, and diagnostic monitors.
