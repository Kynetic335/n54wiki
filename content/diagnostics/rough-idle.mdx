# Rough Idle

> **Source confidence:** High confidence (diagnostic approach). Specifics — verify by VIN/ROM ID.

## What the symptom means

Rough idle is an idle that shakes, hunts up and down, or feels unstable, sometimes with a misfire or near-stall. Idle is a low-load, low-airflow condition where small problems show up clearly — a single weak cylinder or a small vacuum leak that you would never feel at speed becomes obvious at idle.

## Common causes

- **Worn or failing spark plugs / coils** — idle misfire on one or more cylinders.
- **Carbon buildup** on intake valves (direct injection) — disrupts airflow and idle quality.
- **Vacuum leak** — unmetered air upsets the idle mixture; also disrupts boost-control vacuum.
- **VANOS fault** — cam timing not holding target at idle.
- **Failing or mis-coded injectors** — uneven fueling per cylinder.
- **PCV / crankcase ventilation fault** — acts like a vacuum leak.
- **Dirty throttle body / idle control issues.**

## What to log

- Misfire counters **per cylinder**
- [STFT / LTFT](/wiki/logging/stft-ltft) (fuel trims)
- VANOS target vs actual (if available)
- RPM at idle

## What a healthy pattern looks like

- Misfire counters at/near zero at idle.
- Fuel trims small and stable.
- VANOS actual tracking target.
- Steady idle rpm.

## What a bad pattern can suggest

- **One cylinder's misfire counter climbing** → that cylinder's plug/coil/injector.
- **Large positive fuel trims** → unmetered air (vacuum/PCV leak) or lean fueling.
- **VANOS actual not tracking target** → VANOS solenoid/oil/mechanical issue ([VANOS Codes](/wiki/diagnostics/vanos-codes)).
- Idle improving after carbon cleaning → buildup was the cause.

## Mechanical checks first

1. Inspect / replace **spark plugs**; **swap-test coils**.
2. Smoke-test for **vacuum leaks**; inspect **PCV**.
3. Check **VANOS** (oil condition, solenoids).
4. Consider **walnut blasting** if carbon is likely.
5. Verify **injector coding** if injectors were touched.

## Tuning-related causes second

Idle quality is rarely a tuning fix on the N54 — it is almost always hardware (ignition, vacuum, carbon, VANOS). Do not adjust idle/fueling tables to mask a dead coil, a vacuum leak, or carbon buildup.

## When to stop tuning

If idle is rough, fix the mechanical cause before any further tuning. A rough baseline produces misleading logs.
