# Lambda vs AFR

> **Source confidence:** Needs validation

## What it is

Lambda and AFR (air-fuel ratio) both describe mixture. Lambda is normalized (1.0 = stoichiometric), while AFR is fuel-specific (the stoich AFR differs by fuel).

## Why it matters

Lambda is fuel-agnostic, which makes it the safer way to discuss mixture across gasoline and ethanol blends. AFR can mislead if the fuel changes.

## How it works

Lambda 1.0 means exactly enough air for complete combustion of that fuel. Below 1.0 is rich; above 1.0 is lean.

## Common symptoms or failure modes

- Misreading mixture when comparing AFR across different fuels

## What to check in logs

- Lambda / AFR
- STFT / LTFT

## Mechanical checks first

Confirm the wideband/lambda source is healthy before judging mixture.
