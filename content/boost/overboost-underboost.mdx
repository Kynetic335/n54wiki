# Overboost / Underboost

> **Source confidence:** Needs validation

## What it is

Overboost is actual boost exceeding target; underboost is actual falling short. Both trigger DME protection and fault codes.

## Why it matters

Persistent over/underboost indicates a boost-control or mechanical problem and can cause limp mode. Overboost can be dangerous to the engine.

## How it works

The DME compares target and actual; large or sustained deviations set faults and can cut power.

## Common symptoms or failure modes

- Limp mode
- Power cut
- Boost spikes or shortfalls

## What to check in logs

- Boost target vs actual
- WGDC

## Mechanical checks first

Check wastegates, solenoids, vacuum lines, and charge path before tuning.

## Related codes or fault patterns

- Underboost (e.g. 30FF) and boost-deviation codes — verify by scan.
