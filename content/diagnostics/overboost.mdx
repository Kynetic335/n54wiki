# Overboost

> **Source confidence:** High confidence (behavior). Exact thresholds — verify by VIN/ROM ID.

## What the symptom means

Overboost is when actual boost rises **above** the boost the DME is targeting, by more than the allowed margin. The DME treats overboost as dangerous — too much boost for the requested torque raises cylinder pressure and knock risk — so it intervenes, often cutting fuel/throttle and dropping into [limp mode](/wiki/diagnostics/limp-mode).

A short, small spike above target during fast spool can be normal transient behavior. **Sustained** overboost, or a large spike, is the problem.

## Common causes

- **Stuck or slow wastegate** — not opening enough to bleed exhaust, so boost climbs.
- **Boost-control solenoid fault** — commanding the wastegate incorrectly.
- **Vacuum routing problem** — wastegate actuator not seeing the pressure/vacuum it should.
- **Boost-control (WGDC) calibration too aggressive** for the hardware.
- **Mechanical binding** in the wastegate linkage.

## What to log

- [Boost target vs actual](/wiki/logging/boost-target-vs-actual)
- [WGDC](/wiki/logging/wgdc)
- RPM, gear

## What a normal log pattern looks like

- Actual boost rises to target and settles, with at most a small brief overshoot during spool.
- WGDC modulates sensibly to hold target.

## What a bad log pattern looks like

- Actual boost **climbing past target and staying there**, or spiking hard.
- WGDC behavior inconsistent with the result (e.g. commanding the wastegate open but boost still climbing → mechanical wastegate problem).
- A power cut / limp event right after the spike.

## Mechanical checks first

1. Inspect the **wastegate** for free, full movement (no binding/sticking).
2. Check **boost-control solenoids** and their operation.
3. Verify **vacuum routing** to the wastegate actuators.
4. Look for any restriction preventing the wastegate from opening.

## Tuning-related causes second

After hardware is confirmed good:

- A WGDC base/correction calibration that is too aggressive can cause overshoot.
- Boost target changes without matching boost-control tuning can overshoot.

Do **not** raise the overboost threshold to silence the protection. Overboost protection exists to prevent engine damage.

## When to stop tuning

Stop immediately if you see sustained overboost or repeated overboost-triggered cuts. Overboost can damage the engine. Fix the wastegate/solenoid/vacuum side before any further tuning.
