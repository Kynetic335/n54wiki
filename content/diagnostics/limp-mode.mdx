# Limp Mode

> **Source confidence:** High confidence (behavior). Exact triggers/thresholds — verify by VIN/ROM ID.

## What the symptom means

Limp mode (reduced power / "limp home") is a protective state the DME enters after detecting a serious fault. It sharply limits boost and torque — and may close the throttle and hold rpm down — to protect the engine and drivetrain until the car is restarted or the fault is fixed. Limp mode is a **symptom of an underlying fault**, not a problem to silence. The goal is always to find the trigger.

## Common causes (triggers)

- **Boost faults** — over/underboost, boost-control problems ([2ABC / 3100](/wiki/diagnostics/2abc-3100-boost-faults), [30FF](/wiki/diagnostics/30ff-underboost)).
- **Fuel-pressure faults** — HPFP/LPFP, rail-pressure crash ([29DC](/wiki/diagnostics/29dc-fuel-pressure), [2AAF](/wiki/diagnostics/2aaf-fuel-pump-plausibility)).
- **Knock / timing** protection.
- **Throttle / pedal / torque-model** faults ([Throttle Closures](/wiki/diagnostics/throttle-closures)).
- **Sensor faults** feeding bad data (boost/MAP, throttle, etc.).
- Overheating or other protection thresholds.

## What to log / scan

- **Scan for stored codes first** — the code points at the trigger.
- [Boost target vs actual](/wiki/logging/boost-target-vs-actual) + [WGDC](/wiki/logging/wgdc)
- [HPFP / rail pressure](/wiki/logging/hpfp-pressure)
- [Timing corrections](/wiki/logging/timing-corrections)
- [Throttle angle](/wiki/logging/throttle-angle) + pedal

## What a healthy pattern looks like

- No protective cut; boost/fuel/timing/throttle all behave normally through a pull.
- No stored fault codes.

## What a bad pattern can suggest

- A **sudden power cut** lining up with overboost/underboost → boost fault.
- Cut lining up with a **rail-pressure crash** → fuel fault.
- Cut with **sustained timing pull** → knock protection.
- Throttle slamming shut with the pedal flat → torque-model/sensor fault.

## Mechanical checks first

1. **Scan codes** and read freeze-frame.
2. Trace the trigger: boost (smoke test, vacuum, wastegates), fuel (pumps, rail pressure), ignition (plugs/coils), or a sensor.
3. Fix the **root cause**.

## Tuning-related causes second

Never "fix" limp mode by raising/removing the protection threshold or clearing codes without fixing the cause. That hides a real fault and can let damage happen. Address the trigger; protections exist for a reason.

## When to stop tuning

Limp mode means **stop** until the trigger is found and fixed. Do not keep driving hard or tuning around an active protection event.
