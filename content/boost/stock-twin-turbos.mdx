# Stock Twin Turbos

> **Source confidence:** Needs validation

## What it is

The N54 ships with two small parallel turbochargers, each feeding three cylinders. They spool quickly but have a limited airflow ceiling.

## Why it matters

The stock turbos define the safe airflow/boost ceiling. Pushing past it risks heat, knock, and reliability problems.

## How it works

Each turbo is boost-controlled by its own wastegate via WGDC; the DME closes the loop on a boost/MAP sensor.

## Common symptoms or failure modes

- Wastegate rattle as they wear
- Falling boost ceiling on high-mile turbos

## What to check in logs

- Boost target vs actual
- WGDC

## Mechanical checks first

Inspect for wastegate rattle and shaft play before tuning boost.
