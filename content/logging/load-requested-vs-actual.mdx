# Load Requested vs Actual

> **Source confidence:** High confidence (concept). Specific values — verify by VIN/ROM ID.

## What the channels mean

The N54 is torque/load based. **Requested load** is the load the DME is aiming for (derived from the torque request); **actual load** is the load it measures the engine achieving from airflow/manifold pressure. Read together, they tell you whether the engine is delivering what it intends — and, with [boost target vs actual](/wiki/logging/boost-target-vs-actual), whether a shortfall is air-side or a limiter.

## Why it matters

Two very different problems look similar from the driver's seat. Requested vs actual separates them: if **actual lags requested**, the engine is falling short (often a mechanical boost shortfall); if **requested itself is capped**, a limiter is holding power down. Knowing which is which decides whether you smoke-test the car or look at the calibration.

## What normal behavior generally looks like

- Requested and actual load **rise together** through the pull.
- Actual closely tracks requested.
- Behavior repeats pull to pull on a warm car.

## What abnormal behavior can suggest

- **Actual below requested** → boost shortfall: leak, worn turbo, wastegate, or vacuum fault. See [Low Boost](/wiki/diagnostics/low-boost), [30FF](/wiki/diagnostics/30ff-underboost).
- **Requested pinned at a flat ceiling** → a limiter (load/torque cap), not a hardware shortfall. See [Throttle Closures](/wiki/diagnostics/throttle-closures).
- Actual dropping with a **throttle closure** → protective/limiter intervention.

## Common interpretation mistakes

- Confusing a **shortfall** (actual < requested) with a **ceiling** (requested capped) — opposite fixes.
- Reading load without **boost target vs actual** for context.
- Assuming "add boost in the tune" when actual lags requested due to a leak.
- Comparing load across different gears/temps/tunes.
