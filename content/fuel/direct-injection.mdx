# Direct Injection (Fuel)

> **Source confidence:** Needs validation

## What it is

The N54 uses direct injection: a low-pressure pump feeds a high-pressure pump that supplies injectors firing directly into the cylinders.

## Why it matters

The two-stage pump architecture creates specific failure and limit points — especially HPFP capacity and rail pressure hold under load.

## How it works

LPFP lifts fuel from the tank to the HPFP; the HPFP raises it to high rail pressure; the DME targets a rail pressure and commands injector pulse.

## Common symptoms or failure modes

- Long crank
- Rail pressure drop under load
- Carbon buildup on intake valves

## What to check in logs

- Fuel rail pressure target vs actual
- LPFP pressure/duty
- Fuel trims

## Mechanical checks first

Check pump and injector health before assuming a tuning cause.
