# Lambda Target Map

> **Source confidence:** Needs validation

> Educational reference only. This page does not publish tune values.

## What this map controls

The lambda target map sets the commanded air-fuel mixture across load and rpm (typically richer under boost for safety).

## Why it matters

Mixture affects power, knock margin, and component temps. Too lean under boost is dangerous; too rich wastes power and fouls plugs.

## Related log channels

- Lambda/AFR
- STFT/LTFT
- Fuel rail pressure

## What changes are visible in logs

Changes show as a different commanded lambda and measured mixture under load.

## What can go wrong if changed blindly

- Lean under boost (knock/damage)
- Overly rich (power loss, fouling)

## Mechanical problems that can look like a tuning problem

- Fuel-supply limits
- Leaking injectors
- Sensor faults
