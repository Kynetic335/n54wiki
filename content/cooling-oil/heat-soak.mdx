# Heat Soak

> **Source confidence:** Needs validation

## What it is

Heat soak is the buildup of heat in the intercooler, intake, and engine bay after repeated or sustained load, raising intake temps and pulling timing.

## Why it matters

Heat soak explains why later pulls make less power than the first — a heat problem, not a tune problem.

## How it works

Accumulated heat raises IAT and knock risk; the DME pulls timing to protect the engine.

## Common symptoms or failure modes

- IAT climbing pull-to-pull
- Timing pull
- Power drop on repeated runs

## What to check in logs

- IAT
- Timing corrections
- Coolant/oil temp

## Mechanical checks first

Improve heat management (intercooler, cooldown) rather than masking with tuning.
