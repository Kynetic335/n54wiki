# Misfire

> **Source confidence:** High confidence (diagnostic approach). Specifics — verify by VIN/ROM ID.

## What the symptom means

A misfire is a combustion event that fails or burns incompletely in a cylinder. The DME detects it from crankshaft-speed irregularities and counts misfires **per cylinder**, setting a fault code when counts cross a threshold. Misfires feel like a stumble, shake, or sudden power loss, and on the N54 they very commonly appear **under boost**, where cylinder pressure is highest and weak ignition shows up first.

Per-cylinder counts are the most useful clue: a problem on one cylinder points at that cylinder's hardware, while all cylinders misfiring points at something shared (fuel, boost, a leak).

## Common causes

- **Worn or wrong-gap spark plugs** — top cause on tuned cars; spark blows out under boost.
- **Weak / failing ignition coils.**
- **Fuel-supply problems** — rail pressure crash → lean misfire under load.
- **Failing or mis-coded injectors.**
- **Carbon buildup** on intake valves (direct injection).
- **Vacuum / boost leaks** upsetting a cylinder.
- **Over-aggressive ignition timing** causing knock-related misfire (tuning cause, last).

## What to log

- Misfire counters **per cylinder**
- [Timing corrections](/wiki/logging/timing-corrections)
- [Lambda / AFR](/wiki/logging/lambda-afr) and [rail pressure](/wiki/logging/hpfp-pressure)
- RPM, gear, load

## What a normal log pattern looks like

- Misfire counters stay at or near zero through the pull.
- No single cylinder standing out.

## What a bad log pattern looks like

- **Counts climbing on one cylinder** → that cylinder's plug/coil/injector.
- **Counts on all cylinders rising under boost** → shared cause: fueling (rail pressure crash, lean), a boost leak, or too-aggressive timing.
- Misfires arriving exactly when boost peaks → ignition breaking down under pressure (plugs/coils first suspects).

## Mechanical checks first

1. Inspect / replace **spark plugs**; confirm correct gap and heat range.
2. **Swap-test coils** — move a suspect coil to another cylinder and see if the misfire follows.
3. Check **fueling** (rail pressure under load, injector condition/coding).
4. Consider **walnut blasting** if carbon buildup is likely.
5. Smoke-test for **vacuum/boost leaks**.

## Tuning-related causes second

Only after ignition, fueling, and mechanical condition are verified should the calibration's ignition timing be considered. Adding fuel or pulling timing in the tune to mask a misfire from a dead coil or worn plug just hides a hardware fault.

## When to stop tuning

Stop loading the engine if misfires appear under boost — especially if paired with timing pull or a lean condition. Fix the ignition/fuel hardware first.
