# N54 Twin Turbo Layout

> **Source confidence:** Needs validation

## What it is

The N54 uses two small turbochargers in a parallel arrangement, each feeding three cylinders. This reduces lag compared to a single large turbo.

## Why it matters

The twin-turbo layout shapes spool behavior, boost control complexity, and the upgrade paths (hybrids, upgraded twins, or single turbo).

## How it works

Each turbo has its own wastegate. The DME controls boost via wastegate duty cycle (WGDC) and reads a boost/MAP sensor to close the loop.

## Common symptoms or failure modes

- Boost imbalance side-to-side
- Wastegate rattle
- Underboost or overboost

## What to check in logs

- Boost target vs actual
- WGDC

## Mechanical checks first

Inspect wastegate arms, vacuum lines, and diverter valves before tuning boost.
