# LPFP (Low-Pressure Fuel Pump)

> **Source confidence:** Needs validation

## What it is

The LPFP is the in-tank low-pressure pump that supplies fuel to the high-pressure pump. It sets the supply side of the fuel system.

## Why it matters

On higher-power or high-ethanol builds, LPFP supply can become the limit before the HPFP. A weak LPFP starves the HPFP.

## How it works

The LPFP lifts fuel from the tank and maintains low-side pressure to feed the HPFP inlet.

## Common symptoms or failure modes

- Fuel pressure drop at high load
- Lean condition under sustained load
- Limp mode on fuel faults

## What to check in logs

- LPFP pressure
- LPFP duty (if available)
- Fuel rail pressure target vs actual

## Mechanical checks first

Test low-side supply pressure and inspect wiring/connector before tuning.

## Related codes or fault patterns

- Fuel-pressure plausibility faults (e.g. 2AAF) — verify exact code by scan.
