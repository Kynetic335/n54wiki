# 29DC Fuel Pressure

> **Source confidence:** High confidence (commonly reported). Exact code text — verify by scan / VIN/ROM ID.

## What the code means

29DC is a frequently reported N54 **fuel-pressure** fault. In practice it relates to the high-pressure (rail) side not meeting its commanded pressure — the system expected a rail pressure and did not get it. It is strongly associated with **HPFP** weakness and shows up with long crank, rail-pressure crashes under load, and limp mode. Confirm the exact wording on your car — **verify by scan.**

## Common causes

- **Failing / worn HPFP** (most common).
- **Weak LPFP starving** the HPFP, surfacing as a high-side fault.
- Fuel-system **leak-down** or contamination.
- Sensor/wiring fault on the fuel-pressure circuit.

## What to log / scan

- **Scan to confirm 29DC** and read freeze-frame.
- [HPFP / rail pressure target vs actual](/wiki/logging/hpfp-pressure) at start and under load
- [LPFP pressure](/wiki/logging/lpfp-pressure)
- [Lambda / AFR](/wiki/logging/lambda-afr)

## What a healthy pattern looks like

- Rail pressure builds quickly at start and tracks target under load.
- LPFP supply steady.

## What a bad pattern can suggest

- **Rail pressure crashing under load** with 29DC stored → HPFP at its limit ([HPFP Failure](/wiki/diagnostics/hpfp-failure), [Fuel Pressure Crash](/wiki/diagnostics/fuel-pressure-crash)).
- **Slow start-up build** → long crank.
- LPFP sag first → low side is the real cause ([LPFP Failure](/wiki/diagnostics/lpfp-failure)).

## Mechanical checks first

1. Test **rail pressure** behavior (start + load).
2. Verify the **LPFP** is feeding the HPFP.
3. Inspect fuel-pressure **sensor/wiring**.
4. Replace a confirmed-failing HPFP.

## Tuning-related causes second

29DC is a hardware/supply fault, not a tuning value. Do not clear it or lower fuel-pressure targets to limp along — fix the pump/supply.

## When to stop tuning

**Stop** if 29DC appears with a rail-pressure crash and lean lambda. Repair the fuel system before further load.
