# 2ABC / 3100 Boost Faults

> **Source confidence:** High confidence (commonly reported). Exact code text — verify by scan / VIN/ROM ID.

## What the codes mean

2ABC and 3100 are frequently reported N54 **boost-related** fault codes. They indicate the boost system did not behave as the DME expected — a boost-control or boost-deviation problem. Like [30FF](/wiki/diagnostics/30ff-underboost), they are **result codes**: they tell you boost was wrong, not why. On the N54 the cause is usually mechanical (leaks, vacuum, wastegates, solenoids). Confirm exact definitions — **verify by scan.**

## Common causes

- **Boost / charge-path leak** (chargepipe, couplers, intercooler).
- **Vacuum leak** in the boost-control system.
- **Failing wastegates / rattle** or **boost solenoids**.
- **Sensor fault** (boost/MAP) reporting implausible boost.
- Boost-control **calibration** mismatched to hardware (tuning cause, last).

## What to log / scan

- **Scan to confirm the exact code(s)** and read freeze-frame.
- [Boost target vs actual](/wiki/logging/boost-target-vs-actual)
- [WGDC](/wiki/logging/wgdc)
- RPM, gear

## What a healthy pattern looks like

- Actual boost tracks target; WGDC sensible and not pinned.
- Boost/MAP sensor reads plausible values.

## What a bad pattern can suggest

- **Boost short + WGDC maxed** → leak/wastegate ([Low Boost](/wiki/diagnostics/low-boost)).
- **Boost over target** → stuck wastegate/solenoid ([Overboost](/wiki/diagnostics/overboost)).
- **Boost hunting** → oscillation / vacuum / solenoid ([Boost Oscillation](/wiki/diagnostics/boost-oscillation)).
- Implausible sensor reading → boost/MAP sensor fault.

## Mechanical checks first

1. **Smoke test** the charge path.
2. Inspect **vacuum lines** and boost-control components.
3. Check **wastegates** (rattle/play) and **solenoids**.
4. Verify the **boost/MAP sensor** reads plausibly.

## Tuning-related causes second

After the hardware is proven healthy, a boost-control calibration mismatched to the turbos can throw these codes. Address it in the boost-control tuning — never by raising the fault threshold or clearing codes to hide a real deviation.

## When to stop tuning

**Stop** and fix the mechanical/boost-control hardware if these codes appear, especially with limp mode. Tuning cannot seal a leak or fix a stuck wastegate.
