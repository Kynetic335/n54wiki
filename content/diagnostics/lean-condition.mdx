# Lean Condition

> **Source confidence:** High confidence (behavior). Target values — verify by VIN/ROM ID.

## What the symptom means

A lean condition is when there is too little fuel for the amount of air — measured lambda runs **above** the DME's target (or AFR higher than commanded). At idle/cruise a small lean drift may just be a vacuum leak. **Lean under boost is the dangerous one**: it raises combustion temperature and knock risk and can damage the engine quickly.

## Common causes

- **Fuel-supply limit** — [HPFP](/wiki/diagnostics/hpfp-failure) / [LPFP](/wiki/diagnostics/lpfp-failure) cannot hold rail pressure under load (see [Fuel Pressure Crash](/wiki/diagnostics/fuel-pressure-crash)).
- **Higher ethanol content than the tune assumes** — needs more fuel volume.
- **Vacuum / intake leak** — unmetered air (more common at idle/cruise).
- **Failing or clogged injectors.**
- **Faulty O2 / wideband sensor** reporting wrong mixture ([O2 Sensor Faults](/wiki/diagnostics/o2-sensor-faults)).
- **A calibration leaner than the fuel/hardware supports** (tuning cause, addressed carefully).

## What to log

- [Lambda / AFR](/wiki/logging/lambda-afr)
- [STFT / LTFT](/wiki/logging/stft-ltft)
- [HPFP / rail pressure](/wiki/logging/hpfp-pressure) and [LPFP pressure](/wiki/logging/lpfp-pressure)
- RPM, gear, load

## What a healthy pattern looks like

- Lambda sits at the commanded target, including under boost (commonly richer under load for safety).
- Fuel trims small.
- Rail pressure holds target.

## What a bad pattern can suggest

- **Lambda going lean as rail pressure drops** under load → fuel-supply limit; **stop**.
- **Lean with large positive fuel trims** at idle/cruise → unmetered air (vacuum leak).
- Lean only on one bank/cylinder → injector or sensor issue.
- Lean matching a known **high ethanol** fill the tune was not set for → blend mismatch.

## Mechanical checks first

1. Check **rail pressure under load** (HPFP/LPFP capacity).
2. Confirm **actual ethanol content** vs the tune.
3. Smoke-test for **vacuum/intake leaks**.
4. Inspect **injectors** and **O2/wideband** sensor health.

## Tuning-related causes second

Only after fuel supply, blend, leaks, injectors, and sensors are verified should lambda targets in the calibration be revisited. Never command a leaner mixture to chase power past what the fuel system can safely support.

## When to stop tuning

**Stop the pull immediately** on any lean condition under boost. Lean + boost is a fast path to engine damage.
