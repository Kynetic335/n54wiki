# Wastegate Rattle (Diagnosis)

> **Source confidence:** High confidence (behavior). Specifics — verify by VIN/ROM ID.

## What the symptom means

Wastegate rattle is a metallic rattling noise, typically heard at light throttle, idle off-boost, or low rpm, caused by play in the wastegate linkage of worn turbochargers. As the stock turbos age, the wastegate arm and flapper develop slop, and the loose parts rattle when not held shut by boost pressure. It is one of the most common signs of N54 stock-turbo wear.

## Common causes

- **Worn wastegate linkage / flapper** on aging stock turbos (the cause).
- Mistaken for rattle but actually something else (loose heat shield, exhaust component) — confirm the source.

## What to log

Rattle is primarily an **audible/mechanical** diagnosis, but logs help judge whether boost control is also affected:

- [Boost target vs actual](/wiki/logging/boost-target-vs-actual)
- [WGDC](/wiki/logging/wgdc)

## What a healthy pattern looks like

- No rattle off-boost.
- Boost tracks target; WGDC steady and not maxed.

## What a bad pattern can suggest

- Audible rattle at light throttle **plus** inconsistent boost control (boost not holding, WGDC working hard) → significant wastegate wear affecting performance.
- Rattle without boost issues yet → early wear; monitor, plan for turbo service.

## Mechanical checks first

1. Confirm the noise is the **wastegate** (not a heat shield / exhaust rattle).
2. Check **wastegate arm play** on the turbos.
3. Assess severity — rattle worsens as wear increases.

## Tuning-related causes second

Wastegate rattle is **mechanical wear**, not a tuning condition. There is no map that fixes worn linkages. Severe rattle means turbo repair/replacement. Do not try to compensate for failing wastegates in the boost-control calibration.

## When to stop tuning

If rattle is paired with boost-control problems (oscillation, underboost), address the turbos/wastegates before tuning boost. Tuning cannot restore worn hardware.
