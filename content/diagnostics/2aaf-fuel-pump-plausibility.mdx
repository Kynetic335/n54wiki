# 2AAF Fuel Pump Plausibility

> **Source confidence:** High confidence (commonly reported). Exact code text — verify by scan / VIN/ROM ID.

## What the code means

2AAF is a frequently reported N54 **fuel-pump plausibility** fault. "Plausibility" means the DME compared expected fuel-supply behavior against what it measured and found them inconsistent. In practice it is commonly associated with the **low-side / LPFP** supply not delivering what the system expects relative to demand. Confirm the exact wording — **verify by scan.**

## Common causes

- **Weak / failing LPFP** or undersized for the power/ethanol level.
- **Wiring / connector / control** issue on the low-pressure pump circuit.
- Fuel-level sender / strainer / supply restriction.
- A demand the low side cannot meet (high-ethanol/high-power builds).

## What to log / scan

- **Scan to confirm 2AAF** and read freeze-frame.
- [LPFP pressure](/wiki/logging/lpfp-pressure) under load
- [HPFP / rail pressure](/wiki/logging/hpfp-pressure) (downstream effect)
- [Lambda / AFR](/wiki/logging/lambda-afr)

## What a healthy pattern looks like

- Low-side pressure steady; rail pressure tracks target; lambda at target.
- No plausibility code stored.

## What a bad pattern can suggest

- **Low-side pressure sagging** under load with 2AAF stored → LPFP supply limit ([LPFP Failure](/wiki/diagnostics/lpfp-failure)).
- Sag followed by **rail-pressure crash** → low side is starving the HPFP ([Fuel Pressure Crash](/wiki/diagnostics/fuel-pressure-crash)).
- Erratic low-side pressure → wiring/pump fault.

## Mechanical checks first

1. Test **low-side supply** under demand.
2. Inspect **wiring / connector** and pump operation.
3. Confirm **ethanol blend** vs build capacity.
4. Upgrade/replace the LPFP if it cannot keep up.

## Tuning-related causes second

2AAF is a fuel-supply/hardware fault, not a tuning value. Do not clear it or mask it; address the low-side capacity.

## When to stop tuning

**Stop** if 2AAF appears with low-side sag and a rail-pressure crash under boost. Upgrade/repair the fuel supply first.
