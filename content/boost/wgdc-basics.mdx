# WGDC Basics

> **Source confidence:** Needs validation

## What it is

WGDC (wastegate duty cycle) is the percentage command the DME sends to the boost-control solenoids to position the wastegates and hold the boost target.

## Why it matters

WGDC is the primary boost-control output. Reading it against boost target vs actual reveals whether boost control is healthy or fighting a leak/mechanical issue.

## How it works

Higher duty generally holds the wastegate more closed (more boost) within the control strategy; the DME trims it to track the target.

## Common symptoms or failure modes

- WGDC pegged high while boost stays low (suggests a leak or mechanical fault)

## What to check in logs

- WGDC
- Boost target vs actual

## Mechanical checks first

High WGDC with low boost usually means a mechanical/leak problem, not a tune problem.
