# Logging WGDC

> **Source confidence:** High confidence (concept). Specific values — verify by VIN/ROM ID.

## What the channel means

WGDC (wastegate duty cycle) is the percentage command the DME sends to the boost-control solenoids to position the wastegates. It is the **output** of the boost controller — how hard the DME is working to hit its boost target. It is only meaningful when read **alongside** [boost target vs actual](/wiki/logging/boost-target-vs-actual).

## Why it matters

WGDC is the channel that tells you *why* boost is doing what it is doing. Boost can be low for many reasons; WGDC narrows it down. The classic diagnostic: if WGDC is **pinned near maximum and boost is still below target**, the controller is doing everything it can and the air is escaping or the wastegate cannot hold — a **mechanical** problem, not a tuning one.

## What normal behavior generally looks like

- WGDC rises to build boost, then **modulates at a sensible level** to hold target.
- It is **not pinned at its maximum** to maintain boost on a healthy car.
- Smooth, repeatable adjustments.

## What abnormal behavior can suggest

- **WGDC maxed + boost short** → leak, failing wastegate, or worn turbo. Smoke test first.
- **WGDC swinging erratically** with boost hunting → oscillation (vacuum/solenoid/wastegate or over-aggressive control).
- **WGDC commanding the wastegate open but boost still climbing** → stuck wastegate (overboost).
- WGDC behavior that does not match the boost result at all → suspect a control or sensor problem.

## Common interpretation mistakes

- Reading WGDC **without** boost target vs actual — the number alone means little.
- Assuming "raise the WGDC in the tune" fixes low boost when the controller is already maxed (it cannot push past a leak).
- Comparing WGDC across cars/tunes — base and correction strategies differ.
- Confusing normal high WGDC during spool with a problem.
