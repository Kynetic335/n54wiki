# HPFP / LPFP / Port Injection

The N54 uses a **dual-pressure direct injection fuel system**. Understanding how it works — and where it limits your build — is essential for any build beyond Stage 2.

---

## How the N54 Fuel System Works

### Low-Pressure Fuel Pump (LPFP)
The LPFP is an **in-tank electric pump** that draws fuel from the tank and supplies it to the HPFP at low pressure (typically 5–7 bar, roughly 70–100 psi). The LPFP is a high-volume, low-pressure unit.

- Controls fuel flow volume
- Becomes a bottleneck at high ethanol content (ethanol has lower energy density than gasoline, requiring more volume for the same power)
- Located inside the fuel tank

### High-Pressure Fuel Pump (HPFP)
The HPFP is a **cam-driven piston pump** mounted on the engine and driven by the intake camshaft lobe. It takes low-pressure fuel from the LPFP and pressurizes it to high-pressure rail pressure (typically 50–200 bar depending on demand).

- Controls fuel pressure
- Driven at cam speed — output is RPM-dependent
- Bosch HPFP on early N54s is a known failure item (extended BMW warranty applied to many vehicles)
- The HPFP is the fuel system component most directly affecting drivability and WOT fuel delivery

### Fuel Rail and Injectors
The high-pressure rail distributes fuel to the six piezoelectric direct injectors. Injectors spray directly into the combustion chamber.

---

## HPFP Failure Symptoms

- Hard start or crank-no-start when hot
- Misfires or hesitation under boost
- Fuel pressure drop logged at WOT (actual pressure drops below commanded)
- Fault code: **30FF** (high-pressure fuel system pressure too low)

### Diagnosing HPFP Health
Log **HPFP actual pressure vs. commanded pressure** on a WOT pull. A healthy pump holds actual pressure within a close margin of commanded. A drop of more than a few bar under load, or a sharp dive at high RPM, indicates the pump is struggling.

## HPFP Upgrade Options

| Option | Notes |
|---|---|
| BMW OEM replacement | Reliable; same spec as original |
| Bosch/Siemens OEM supply | The actual manufacturer; same part |
| Vargas Turbo HPFP upgrade | Upgraded internals for higher flow capacity; popular for high-power builds |
| MHD HPFP kit | Aftermarket option with community validation |

For most Stage 1–Stage 2+ builds, a healthy stock HPFP is adequate. Upgraded HPFPs become relevant when pushing the fueling system hard (high boost, high ethanol content, or large injectors).

---

## LPFP Upgrade

The stock LPFP (typically a Bosch unit in the ~220–260 LPH range) is adequate for pump gas through Stage 2. At E30+, the increased fuel volume demand on higher-power builds can exceed its capacity.

### When to upgrade:
- Running E50 or higher ethanol content
- Running a high-power single-turbo build
- LPFP showing signs of fatigue (hard starts, erratic fuel pressure at idle)

### Common LPFP upgrades:
- **AEM 320 LPH** — popular, well-documented N54 fitment
- **Walbro 450 LPH** — higher output for very high fuel demand builds
- **Titan Motorsports LPFP** — another well-regarded option

Always use a quality fuel pump installation kit with a new strainer/filter sock.

---

## Port Injection

The N54 is a **direct injection (DI) only** engine from the factory. At high power levels, particularly with high ethanol content, the DI system can reach its flow limits. Port injection (PI) adds a supplementary low-pressure injection system that sprays fuel into the intake port, **before** the intake valve.

### Benefits of port injection:
- Additional fuel volume capacity beyond DI limits
- Intake valve cleaning (port injection wets the valves, helping prevent carbon buildup)
- More consistent fuel distribution at high loads

### Port injection kits:
- **MHD Port Injection Kit** — designed specifically for the N54; integrates with MHD tune
- **BEF (Back-End Flash) / JB4 port injection** — available through Burger Motorsports ecosystem
- Custom fabrication for one-off builds

### When is PI needed?
Port injection is generally not required until you are pushing a build with very high fuel demand — typically high-power single-turbo builds on heavy ethanol content. Most hybrid twin builds do not require PI unless targeting extreme power numbers.
