# N20 TMAP Conversion

> **Source confidence:** Needs validation

## What it is

An N20 TMAP conversion adapts a higher-range temperature/manifold-absolute-pressure sensor from the N20 engine to extend the N54's readable boost range. Requires matching calibration scaling.

## Why it matters

It is a common way to read higher boost than the stock sensor allows on big builds — but the scaling must be set correctly or boost is misread.

## How it works

The TMAP combines temperature and pressure sensing; the calibration must apply the correct scaling for the substituted sensor.

## Common symptoms or failure modes

- Misread boost/IAT if scaling is wrong

## What to check in logs

- Boost target vs actual
- IAT

## Mechanical checks first

Confirm correct sensor and scaling; mismatch produces wrong boost/IAT readings.
