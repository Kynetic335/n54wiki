# No Start

> **Source confidence:** High confidence (approach). Specifics — verify by VIN/ROM ID.

## What the symptom means

A no-start is when the engine fails to run. Split it into two cases:

- **Cranks but will not fire** — the starter turns the engine but it never catches. Usually fuel, spark, or a key signal is missing.
- **Will not crank** — the engine does not turn over. Usually electrical (battery, starter, safety interlocks).

The N54 needs three things to start: fuel pressure, spark, and correct sensor/sync signals. Missing any one prevents starting.

## Common causes

- **Fuel pressure** — failed HPFP or LPFP (no/low rail pressure). Common N54 cause of crank-no-fire.
- **Fuel system leak-down** or empty/contaminated fuel.
- **Ignition** — multiple coil/plug failures (less common as a total no-start).
- **Crank/cam sensor or sync fault** — DME cannot time injection/spark.
- **Battery / starter / electrical** — for no-crank.
- **Security / immobilizer or other interlocks.**

## What to log / scan

- **Scan for fault codes first** — fastest path on a no-start.
- [HPFP / rail pressure](/wiki/logging/hpfp-pressure) and [LPFP pressure](/wiki/logging/lpfp-pressure) during cranking
- Battery voltage / cranking
- Crank/cam sensor signals if available

## What a healthy pattern looks like

- Rail pressure builds during cranking to its start target.
- Sensors report valid crank/cam signals.
- Battery holds adequate voltage while cranking.

## What a bad pattern can suggest

- **No rail pressure build while cranking** → fuel-supply failure (HPFP/LPFP).
- **No/low battery voltage or no crank** → electrical.
- **Missing crank/cam signal** → sensor/sync fault.
- Stored fuel-pressure codes ([29DC](/wiki/diagnostics/29dc-fuel-pressure), [2AAF](/wiki/diagnostics/2aaf-fuel-pump-plausibility)) → fuel system.

## Mechanical checks first

1. **Scan codes.**
2. Check **fuel pressure** during cranking (HPFP/LPFP).
3. Check **battery / starter / grounds**.
4. Check **crank/cam sensors** and connectors.
5. Confirm fuel quantity/quality.

## Tuning-related causes second

A no-start is essentially never a tuning value to adjust — it is hardware, electrical, or a sensor/sync problem. Do not attempt to "tune around" a no-start.

## When to stop tuning

A no-start car is not tunable. Restore reliable starting (fuel/spark/signals) before any tuning work.
