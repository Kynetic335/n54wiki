# N54 Direct Injection

> **Source confidence:** Needs validation

## What it is

Direct injection (DI) sprays fuel directly into the combustion chamber at high pressure, rather than into the intake port. The N54 is direct-injection only from the factory.

## Why it matters

DI sets the fueling architecture: a low-pressure pump (LPFP) feeds a high-pressure pump (HPFP) that supplies the injectors. Fuel pressure under load is a common tuning limit.

## How it works

The LPFP lifts fuel from the tank; the HPFP raises it to high rail pressure; injectors fire directly into each cylinder. The DME targets a rail pressure and adjusts injector timing.

## Common symptoms or failure modes

- Long crank
- Fuel pressure drop under load
- Carbon buildup on intake valves (no port fuel to wash them)

## What to check in logs

- Fuel rail pressure (target vs actual)
- LPFP pressure/duty
- Fuel trims

## Mechanical checks first

Check HPFP/LPFP health and injector condition before assuming a tuning cause for fueling faults.
