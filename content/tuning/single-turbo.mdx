# Single Turbo

A single-turbo conversion replaces the N54's twin IHI turbos with a single large turbocharger. This is the highest-power path for the N54 platform and typically requires significant supporting modifications.

---

## Why Single Turbo?

Single turbo conversions are chosen when:

- The target power level exceeds what hybrid twins can produce
- The builder wants the simplicity and tuning flexibility of a single large turbo
- The specific power band (more top-end, sustained boost) suits the application

Single-turbo builds generally have more top-end power than twin setups but sacrifice some low-RPM response and spool time.

---

## System Overview

A typical N54 single-turbo conversion involves:

- **Custom or kit exhaust manifold** — combines all 6 cylinders to a single collector for the turbo
- **Downpipe** — custom or kit
- **Charge piping** — new intercooler plumbing, often with a larger FMIC
- **Oil and coolant lines** — turbo requires oil feed/return and ideally coolant lines
- **BOV / recirculation valve** — BOV or compressor bypass valve
- **Turbo manifold adapter / V-band** — flanges to the turbo

## Popular Manifold Options

### Vargas Turbo Technologies (VTT)
VTT offers an N54 single-turbo manifold kit with fitment for a range of turbocharger sizes. Widely used in the community with documented results.

### Active Autowerke
AAW offers a single-turbo manifold kit with various turbo options. Good community support.

### Custom fabrication
Some builders fabricate custom manifolds for specific turbo orientations or clearance requirements. Requires a competent fabricator familiar with N54 packaging.

## Turbo Selection

Turbo selection depends entirely on your power target, RPM range, and fuel strategy. Key considerations:

- **Frame size:** Larger turbos make more peak power but spool later
- **Compressor map:** Should cover your target flow range efficiently
- **Turbine sizing:** Must pass exhaust flow without excessive back pressure
- **Bearing type:** Ball bearing turbos spool faster; journal bearing units are cheaper and durable
- **Wastegate:** Internal or external. External wastegate gives more precise boost control at high power
- **Popular frame sizes used on N54:** GTX3076, GTX3582, GTX3071, Borg Warner EFR series, Precision Turbo units — selection varies widely by power goal

> **Always involve your tuner in turbo selection.** Power goals, fuel availability, and cam/fueling limitations all affect what's appropriate for your build.

---

## Bottom End Considerations

The N54's closed-deck block and stock forged internals are reasonably strong. However:

- At higher power levels, the risk of engine damage from boost spikes, lean conditions, or knock increases dramatically
- A **built bottom end** (forged pistons, rods, or a stroker kit) is recommended at aggressive power targets
- Head gasket upgrades and head studs become relevant at higher boost and cylinder pressure

There is no universal "safe" power limit for a stock bottom end. This depends on tune quality, fuel, tune file, and how the car is driven. Work with a tuner who understands your goals.

## Fueling for Single Turbo

Higher power demands higher fuel flow. Common single-turbo fueling strategies:

| Strategy | When used |
|---|---|
| Upgraded HPFP + LPFP | Lower-power single-turbo builds |
| Port injection (PI) kit | High power with ethanol; bypasses DI limitations |
| Flex fuel sensor | Allows the tune to adapt between pump gas and ethanol |

See [HPFP / LPFP / Port Injection](/fueling/hpfp-lpfp-port-injection) for fueling system detail.

---

## Tune Requirements

Single-turbo builds require a **fully custom tune** from a tuner with N54 single-turbo experience. Key areas that differ from twin-turbo calibration:

- Boost control strategy (external wastegate requires different WGDC approach)
- Fueling at higher power — may require PI calibration
- Spool ramp targets
- Throttle body and torque model adjustments

Do not run any twin-turbo map on a single-turbo car.
