# MHD Log Channel Guide

This reference covers the most important MHD logging channels for N54 builds. Use it to set up your log profiles and interpret what your data means.

---

## Essential Channels (Always Log)

These channels should be in every log, every pull.

### Timing Corrections (Cylinder 1–6)
`Ignition correction cyl 1` through `Ignition correction cyl 6`

**What it is:** The degree of timing retard the ECU applies to each cylinder in response to detected knock or borderline detonation.

| Reading | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 0° to –1° | Normal; expected on first few pulls |
| –2° to –3° | Monitor closely; may be within tolerance but worth review |
| –4° or more | Concern — pull over, review logs, do not continue WOT pulls |
| Persistent –1° or more on same cyl | Address the cause before continuing |

Consistent corrections on a specific cylinder often indicate a plug, coil, or injector issue on that cylinder.

### HPFP Pressure Actual vs. Requested
`High pressure fuel pump actual (bar)` and `High pressure fuel pump requested (bar)`

**What it is:** Commanded vs. actual rail pressure. A healthy pump tracks the requested value closely.

| Reading | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Actual ≈ Requested | Pump healthy |
| Actual drops 5–10 bar below requested under load | Pump fatigue; monitor |
| Actual drops 15+ bar or sharp dive | Pump failing; do not continue WOT pulls |

See [HPFP / LPFP / Port Injection](/fueling/hpfp-lpfp-port-injection) for pump replacement guidance.

### Boost Pressure Actual vs. Requested
`Charge air pressure actual` and `Charge air pressure setpoint`

**What it is:** Target boost vs. actual boost pressure (typically in mbar or bar absolute/relative depending on MHD version).

| Pattern | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Actual matches setpoint | Boost control functioning correctly |
| Actual oscillates around setpoint | Possible wastegate or solenoid issue |
| Actual undershoots significantly | Underboost — check solenoids, check for boost leaks |
| Actual overshoots setpoint | Overboost — stop pulling; can trigger underboost protection codes |

### Intake Air Temperature (IAT)
`Intake air temperature` or `Charge air temperature`

**What it is:** Temperature of the air entering the engine (post-intercooler). High IAT causes the ECU to reduce timing and boost as a self-protection measure.

| Reading | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Within ~10–15°C of ambient | Intercooler functioning well |
| 20–30°C above ambient | Some heat soak; acceptable |
| 40°C+ above ambient | Significant heat soak; intercooler upgrade warranted |

## Fuel System Channels

### Low Pressure Fuel Pump Supply
`Low pressure fuel pump feedback` or `Fuel pressure low side`

Monitors the supply side to the HPFP. If LPFP output drops, the HPFP cannot maintain rail pressure regardless of pump condition.

### Lambda / Air-Fuel Ratio
`Lambda actual` (or `AFR actual` if your MHD version shows it)

| Range | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 0.85–0.90 λ (12.5–13.2 AFR) at WOT | Normal, slightly rich — expected under boost |
| 0.80 λ or richer | Very rich; may indicate fueling calibration issue |
| 0.95 λ or leaner at WOT | Lean at WOT — stop pulling; this is dangerous |

## Knock and Combustion

### Engine Knock Sensor (Raw Signal)
`Knock signal cyl 1–6` — raw sensor signal, used in conjunction with corrections to understand where the knock event is occurring.

---

## RPM and Load

### Engine RPM
`Engine speed (RPM)`

Essential context for all other channels. Always log this.

### Throttle Pedal Position
`Throttle pedal position (%)`

Confirms the driver is actually at full throttle during a pull. Reviewing logs from a "WOT pull" where TPS was at 95% rather than 100% explains lower than expected boost or power.

---

## Additional Useful Channels

| Channel | Why log it |
|---|---|
| `Oil temperature` | Confirms engine at operating temp; high oil temp under sustained load is a concern |
| `Coolant temperature` | Overtemperature immediately stops pulls |
| `WGDC duty cycle front/rear` | Helps diagnose boost control issues |
| `Torque reduction active` | Shows when DSC/DME is pulling power |
| `Misfires (cyl 1–6)` | Confirms misfire events and which cylinder |

---

## Setting Up an MHD Log Profile

1. In MHD → Logs → Custom Profile
2. Add all channels from the essential list above
3. For a standard road log, 10 Hz sample rate is generally sufficient
4. Save the profile and load it before every pull session
