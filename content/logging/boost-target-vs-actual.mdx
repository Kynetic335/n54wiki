# Boost Target vs Actual

> **Source confidence:** High confidence (concept). Specific values — verify by VIN/ROM ID.

## What the channels mean

These two channels are read together:

- **Boost target** — the manifold pressure the DME is requesting based on load/torque demand.
- **Boost actual** — the manifold pressure the boost/MAP sensor actually reports.

The relationship between them — do they match, does actual fall short, does it overshoot — is the foundation of boost diagnosis on the N54.

## Why it matters

Almost every boost complaint ("flat", "surging", "limp mode") is answered first by these two lines plus [WGDC](/wiki/logging/wgdc). Target vs actual tells you *whether* there is a problem; WGDC and the mechanical checks tell you *why*.

## What normal behavior generally looks like

- Actual rises during spool and then **tracks target closely** for the rest of the pull.
- At most a small, brief overshoot during fast spool, which settles.
- Behavior repeats pull to pull on a warm car.

## What abnormal behavior can suggest

- **Actual below target** → underboost. With WGDC pinned high, strongly suggests a leak or wastegate that cannot hold. See [Low Boost](/wiki/diagnostics/low-boost), [30FF](/wiki/diagnostics/30ff-underboost).
- **Actual above target (sustained)** → overboost; stuck wastegate, solenoid, or vacuum fault. See [Overboost](/wiki/diagnostics/overboost).
- **Actual hunting around target** → oscillation; vacuum/solenoid/wastegate or over-aggressive control. See [Boost Oscillation](/wiki/diagnostics/boost-oscillation).
- **Actual building then falling** mid-pull → a leak opening under pressure.

## Common interpretation mistakes

- Reading actual **without** WGDC — you lose the "is the controller maxed?" clue.
- Treating normal brief spool overshoot as a fault.
- Comparing boost between different gears, IAT, or fuel — not apples to apples.
- Assuming a low number means "needs more boost in the tune" when it is really a leak.
