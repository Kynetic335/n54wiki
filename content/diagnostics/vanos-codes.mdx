# VANOS Codes

> **Source confidence:** High confidence (behavior). Exact code text — verify by scan / VIN/ROM ID.

## What the codes mean

VANOS is BMW's variable camshaft timing system. The DME commands a target cam angle (intake and exhaust) and watches the actual position. A **VANOS code** is set when actual cannot reach or hold target within tolerance. These codes cause rough running, performance loss, and sometimes limp behavior — and on the N54 they are usually a **mechanical / oil** problem, not a tuning one. Exact code numbers vary; **verify by scan.**

## Common causes

- **Dirty or failing VANOS solenoids** (very common) — cannot route oil to move the cam.
- **Low or dirty oil / wrong oil** — VANOS is oil-actuated.
- **Mechanical VANOS wear** (units/seals).
- Sensor / wiring issues affecting cam position reading.

## What to log / scan

- **Scan for the specific VANOS code(s).**
- VANOS **target vs actual** position (intake and exhaust) if available
- RPM, oil temp for context

## What a healthy pattern looks like

- Actual cam position **tracks target** at idle and through the rev range.
- No stored VANOS codes.

## What a bad pattern can suggest

- **Actual not reaching/holding target** → solenoid, oil, or mechanical fault.
- Codes that **clear after solenoid cleaning / oil service** → confirms the cause.
- Worse when oil is old/dirty → oil-related.

## Mechanical checks first

1. Check **oil level/condition**; confirm correct-spec oil and a recent change.
2. **Clean or replace VANOS solenoids.**
3. Inspect for **mechanical VANOS wear** if solenoids/oil are good.
4. Re-scan after service to confirm.

## Tuning-related causes second

VANOS cam-timing tables exist, but a VANOS **code** is almost always hardware/oil — fix that first. Do not change cam-timing tables to suppress a deviation fault caused by a dirty solenoid or old oil.

## When to stop tuning

Resolve VANOS codes before tuning cam timing. The DME cannot hit commanded cam targets if the hardware cannot move the cams.
