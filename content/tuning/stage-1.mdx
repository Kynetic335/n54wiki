# Stage 1

Stage 1 is the entry point for N54 tuning. It typically refers to a **software tune with no or minimal bolt-on hardware modifications** — the goal is extracting power that the stock hardware can safely support.

---

## What "Stage 1" Means on the N54

There's no universal industry standard for stage definitions. On the N54, Stage 1 most commonly refers to:

- ECU tune (via MHD Flasher or equivalent) **with stock hardware** or minor additions
- No downpipe replacement required
- Stock turbochargers, intercooler, and fueling

Some tuners define Stage 1 as allowing an intake, while others reserve that for Stage 2. Clarify with your tuner.

---

## Typical Hardware Baseline (Stage 1)

| Component | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Downpipe | Stock catted acceptable |
| Intercooler | Stock TMIC acceptable |
| Charge pipes | **Aluminum highly recommended** |
| Fuel pump | Stock HPFP acceptable (must be healthy) |
| Spark plugs | **Iridium required**, gapped to 0.020–0.022" |
| Boost solenoids | Should be functional; replace if worn |

---

## Expected Power Range

Power output at Stage 1 depends heavily on fuel quality, ambient conditions, and the specific tune. General community expectations on **93 octane**:

- Crank power increase: meaningful improvement over stock
- Typical wheel power range (93 oct): ~320–360 whp / ~340–380 wtq

> These are **general community ranges**, not guarantees. Exact numbers depend on your specific car, tune file, and conditions. Dyno numbers vary by type (Dynajet vs. Mustang, etc.).

On **91 octane**, expect the tune to pull more timing and deliver modestly less power. On **E30 or higher ethanol blends**, Stage 1 power increases considerably — the ECU has more headroom for timing and boost.

---

## Fuel Compatibility

| Fuel | Stage 1 viability | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 91 octane | Viable, limited gains | Tune will be conservative |
| 93 octane | Standard baseline | Most Stage 1 tunes target 93 |
| E30 blend | Excellent | More timing headroom, more power |
| E85 | Advanced | Requires fueling hardware check |

---

## What to Watch in Datalogs

After a Stage 1 tune, log every WOT pull for the first 5–10 pulls:

- **Timing corrections (cyl 1–6):** Should be close to 0°. Consistent negative corrections mean knock — pull over immediately.
- **HPFP actual vs. requested pressure:** Should track closely. A drop under load indicates pump stress.
- **Boost:** Should hit target consistently. Spikes followed by drops suggest solenoid or wastegate issues.
- **Intake air temperature (IAT):** High IAT causes the ECU to retard timing. Log IAT to understand heat soak.

See [Timing Corrections / Knock](/datalogging/timing-knock) and [Boost Control / WGDC](/datalogging/boost-wgdc) for detailed guidance.

Stage 1 is often a starting point, not a destination. Once you're comfortable with Stage 1 and have reviewed logs, the natural progression is:
