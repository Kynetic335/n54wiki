# Logging Timing Problems

> **Source confidence:** Needs validation

## What it is

Timing problems show up as negative timing corrections (knock pull) under load, sometimes localized to one cylinder.

## Why it matters

Persistent timing pull risks damage and signals fuel quality, heat, plug, or mechanical issues.

## How it works

Knock detection retards timing; the log shows how much and on which cylinders.

## Common symptoms or failure modes

- Negative corrections under load
- One cylinder worse than others

## What to check in logs

- Timing corrections by cylinder
- IAT
- Lambda/AFR

## Mechanical checks first

Check fuel/blend, heat, plugs, and coils; a single-cylinder issue often means hardware.
