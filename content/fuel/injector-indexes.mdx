# Injector Indexes

> **Source confidence:** Needs validation

## What it is

An injector index is a value (printed on the injector) describing its flow characteristics so the DME can compensate for unit-to-unit variation.

## Why it matters

Correct indexing keeps fueling accurate cylinder-to-cylinder. Wrong indexes cause uneven fueling and rough running.

## How it works

Each injector carries an index; the DME applies the matching compensation. Replacing injectors requires entering the correct indexes.

## Common symptoms or failure modes

- Rough idle after injector swap
- Uneven fuel trims
- Misfires

## What to check in logs

- Fuel trims
- Misfire counters by cylinder

## Mechanical checks first

Confirm indexes were entered correctly after any injector work.
