# Coil Failure

> **Source confidence:** High confidence (behavior). Specifics — verify by VIN/ROM ID.

## What the symptom means

The N54 uses one coil per cylinder (coil-on-plug). A weak or failing coil cannot deliver a strong enough spark, and the problem shows up most under boost, where high cylinder pressure makes the spark hardest to sustain. The result is a misfire on that cylinder, often felt as a stumble under load. Coils are a normal N54 wear item.

## Common causes

- **Worn / degraded coil** firing weakly (the failure).
- **Bad connector / wiring** to the coil.
- Coil stressed by **worn plugs / wrong gap** (fix plugs too).

## What to log

- Misfire counters **per cylinder**
- [Timing corrections](/wiki/logging/timing-corrections) (knock vs ignition misfire context)
- RPM, gear, load

## What a healthy pattern looks like

- Misfire counters near zero, including under boost.
- No single cylinder standing out.

## What a bad pattern can suggest

- **One cylinder's misfire count climbing under boost** → that coil (or plug) on that cylinder.
- Misfire that **follows the coil** when you move it to another cylinder → confirmed coil.
- All cylinders misfiring → look past coils to fueling/boost/shared causes.

## Mechanical checks first

1. **Swap-test**: move the suspect coil to another cylinder; if the misfire follows, the coil is bad.
2. Inspect the **connector / wiring**.
3. Check the **spark plug** on that cylinder (gap/wear) — coils and plugs work together.

## Tuning-related causes second

Coil failure is hardware. Do not add fuel or pull timing in the tune to hide a coil misfire — replace the coil. Masking it leaves a misfiring cylinder under boost.

## When to stop tuning

Stop loading the engine if a coil misfire appears under boost. Replace the failing coil (and plug if due) before tuning.
