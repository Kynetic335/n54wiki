# HPFP Failure

> **Source confidence:** High confidence (well-documented N54 issue). Pressure values — verify by VIN/ROM ID.

## What the symptom means

The HPFP (high-pressure fuel pump) raises fuel to the high rail pressure direct injection needs. When it weakens or fails, it can no longer build or hold that pressure — especially at startup and under load. HPFP problems are one of the most documented N54 issues and the cause behind many long-crank, lean-under-load, and limp-mode complaints.

## Common causes

- **Worn / failing HPFP** internals (the failure itself).
- A **weak LPFP starving** the HPFP, which looks like HPFP failure ([LPFP Failure](/wiki/diagnostics/lpfp-failure)).
- Fuel contamination affecting the pump.

## What to log

- [HPFP / rail pressure target vs actual](/wiki/logging/hpfp-pressure) — at start and under load
- [LPFP pressure](/wiki/logging/lpfp-pressure) — to confirm the low side is feeding it
- [Lambda / AFR](/wiki/logging/lambda-afr) — lean under load is the danger

## What a healthy pattern looks like

- Rail pressure builds quickly at start and **tracks target** through the whole pull.
- LPFP supply steady.
- Lambda stays at target.

## What a bad pattern can suggest

- **Rail pressure holding then crashing** under load → HPFP at/over its limit ([Fuel Pressure Crash](/wiki/diagnostics/fuel-pressure-crash)).
- **Slow/low pressure build at start** → long crank ([Long Crank](/wiki/diagnostics/long-crank)).
- Crash paired with **lean lambda** → dangerous; stop.
- If LPFP also sags first, the **low side** may be the real cause.

## Mechanical checks first

1. Test **rail pressure hold** under load and at start.
2. Verify **LPFP** is feeding the HPFP adequately (rule out low-side cause).
3. Scan for fuel-pressure codes ([29DC](/wiki/diagnostics/29dc-fuel-pressure)).
4. Replace a confirmed-failing HPFP.

## Tuning-related causes second

The tune does not cause HPFP failure; it can **expose** a marginal pump by demanding more fuel. The fix is fuel-system capacity (replacement/upgraded HPFP, supporting LPFP), not dialing back targets to limp along on a dying pump.

## When to stop tuning

**Stop immediately** if rail pressure crashes and lambda goes lean. Fix/replace the pump before any further load.
