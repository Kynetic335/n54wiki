# Boost Oscillation

> **Source confidence:** High confidence (behavior). Specifics — verify by VIN/ROM ID.

## What the symptom means

Boost oscillation (also called boost hunting or surging) is when actual boost cannot settle — it bounces above and below target repeatedly instead of holding a steady line. The driver often feels it as a surging or pulsing sensation under load. It indicates the boost-control loop is unstable or fighting something it cannot control.

## Common causes

- **Vacuum leak** in the boost-control system — the wastegate actuator gets an inconsistent signal.
- **Failing boost-control solenoid** — erratic actuation.
- **Worn wastegate / rattle** — slop in the linkage makes position unstable.
- **Diverter valve fluttering / leaking**.
- **Boost-control (WGDC) calibration too aggressive** — closed-loop correction over-reacts and hunts (a true tuning cause, but confirm mechanically first).

## What to log

- [Boost target vs actual](/wiki/logging/boost-target-vs-actual)
- [WGDC](/wiki/logging/wgdc)
- RPM, gear

## What a normal log pattern looks like

- Actual boost converges to target and **holds a steady line**.
- WGDC settles, making small smooth adjustments.

## What a bad log pattern looks like

- Actual boost **sawing up and down** around target.
- WGDC **swinging back and forth** trying to catch up, often out of phase with boost — a controller that is hunting.

If WGDC is erratic *and* you can find a vacuum/solenoid/wastegate fault, the hardware is the cause. If hardware is proven good and the oscillation remains, the boost-control calibration is the likely cause.

## Mechanical checks first

1. **Smoke test** and inspect vacuum lines / reservoirs for leaks.
2. Test the **boost-control solenoids**.
3. Check the **wastegate** for play / rattle.
4. Inspect **diverter valves**.

## Tuning-related causes second

After hardware is confirmed healthy, oscillation can be a closed-loop boost-control tuning issue: correction gains too aggressive cause the loop to overshoot and hunt. This is addressed in the boost-control calibration — see [WGDC Correction Map](/wiki/maps/wgdc-correction) — but only once leaks and failing parts are ruled out.

## When to stop tuning

Stop and inspect hardware if oscillation appears with any sign of a vacuum/solenoid/wastegate fault. Do not paper over a mechanical leak by softening the controller.
