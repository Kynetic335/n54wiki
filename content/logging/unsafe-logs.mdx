# Unsafe Logs

> **Source confidence:** High confidence (safety practice). Specific thresholds — verify by VIN/ROM ID.

## What the channel/topic means

"Unsafe logs" is not a single channel — it is the practice of recognizing when a pull should be stopped because the engine is at risk or the data is invalid. A log taken with knock present, fuel pressure crashing, overheating, or on a cold/faulty car either endangers the engine or produces conclusions you cannot trust.

## Why it matters

Acting on an unsafe or invalid log leads to wrong fixes and, worse, real damage. The single most protective habit in tuning and diagnosis is knowing the conditions that mean **abort the pull now** rather than "see if it holds."

## What normal (safe) behavior generally looks like

- Warm engine, no active faults, healthy maintenance.
- [Boost target vs actual](/wiki/logging/boost-target-vs-actual) tracking; [lambda](/wiki/logging/lambda-afr) at target; [rail pressure](/wiki/logging/hpfp-pressure) holding; little/no [timing pull](/wiki/logging/timing-corrections); temps in range.
- Repeatable, comparable pulls.

## What abnormal (stop-now) behavior can suggest

Stop the pull immediately if you see any of:

- **Knock / sustained timing pull** → [Timing Problems](/wiki/logging/timing-problems), [Timing Corrections](/wiki/diagnostics/timing-corrections).
- **Rail-pressure crash with lean lambda** → [Fuel Pressure Crash](/wiki/diagnostics/fuel-pressure-crash), [Fuel Limits](/wiki/logging/fuel-limits).
- **Overboost** or boost control clearly failing → [Overboost](/wiki/diagnostics/overboost), [Boost Control Problems](/wiki/logging/boost-control-problems).
- **Rising coolant/oil temp** → [Coolant Temp](/wiki/logging/coolant-temp), [Oil Temp](/wiki/logging/oil-temp).
- Cold engine, active fault codes, or a known mechanical issue → data is invalid anyway.

## Common interpretation mistakes

- "Just one more pull" after seeing knock or a pressure crash.
- Trusting data from a **cold** or **faulted** car.
- Logging on public roads instead of a safe, legal setting (e.g. a dyno).
- Treating a mechanical fault (leak, worn pump, bad coil) as something to tune around instead of fix.
