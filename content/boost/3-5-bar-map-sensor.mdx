# 3.5 Bar MAP Sensor

> **Source confidence:** Needs validation

## What it is

A higher-range (e.g. 3.5 bar) MAP sensor extends the boost range the DME can read beyond the stock sensor's ceiling. Verify exact ranges by source.

## Why it matters

High-boost builds can exceed the stock sensor range; the DME then cannot read true manifold pressure, requiring a higher-range sensor and matching calibration.

## How it works

The sensor reports manifold pressure; a higher range plus correct scaling lets the DME interpret higher boost accurately.

## Common symptoms or failure modes

- Boost reading pegged/clipped at the stock sensor ceiling on high-boost builds

## What to check in logs

- Boost target vs actual (watch for clipping)

## Mechanical checks first

Sensor scaling must match the hardware; mismatched scaling misreads boost.
