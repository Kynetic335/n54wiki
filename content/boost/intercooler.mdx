# Intercooler

> **Source confidence:** Needs validation

## What it is

The intercooler (FMIC/TMIC depending on chassis) cools compressed air before it enters the engine, reducing intake temps and knock risk.

## Why it matters

Hot charge air raises knock risk and timing pull. An undersized or heat-soaked intercooler limits sustained power.

## How it works

Air-to-air cores transfer heat from the charge air to ambient airflow.

## Common symptoms or failure modes

- Rising IAT under sustained load
- Timing pull from heat
- Power drop after repeated pulls

## What to check in logs

- IAT (intake air temp)
- Timing corrections

## Mechanical checks first

Check for blockage/damage and adequate sizing before chasing tune changes.
