# Unsafe Logs

> **Source confidence:** Needs validation

## What it is

An unsafe log is one taken under conditions that risk the engine or produce unreliable data — cold engine, knock present, fuel pressure crashing, or overheating.

## Why it matters

Acting on an unsafe or invalid log leads to wrong conclusions and possible damage. Knowing when to stop is part of safe tuning.

## How it works

If a log shows knock, pressure crash, or overheating, the correct response is to stop and fix the cause, not to keep pulling.

## Common symptoms or failure modes

- Knock/timing pull
- Fuel pressure crash
- Overheating during the log

## What to check in logs

- Timing corrections
- Fuel rail pressure
- Coolant/oil temp

## Mechanical checks first

Stop immediately and address the mechanical cause if these appear.
