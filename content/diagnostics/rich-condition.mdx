# Rich Condition

> **Source confidence:** High confidence (behavior). Target values — verify by VIN/ROM ID.

## What the symptom means

A rich condition is when there is too much fuel for the air — measured lambda runs **below** the DME's target (or AFR lower than commanded). Rich running is generally less immediately dangerous than lean, but it wastes power, fouls spark plugs, can wash cylinders, and usually signals a fueling or sensor problem.

## Common causes

- **Leaking injectors** — delivering more fuel than commanded.
- **Faulty O2 / wideband sensor** reporting wrong mixture ([O2 Sensor Faults](/wiki/diagnostics/o2-sensor-faults)).
- **Wrong injector coding** after a swap.
- **Excess fuel from a calibration** richer than needed (tuning cause).
- **Mechanical issues** affecting combustion/sensing.

## What to log

- [Lambda / AFR](/wiki/logging/lambda-afr)
- [STFT / LTFT](/wiki/logging/stft-ltft)
- [HPFP / rail pressure](/wiki/logging/hpfp-pressure)
- RPM, gear, load

## What a healthy pattern looks like

- Lambda at the commanded target.
- Fuel trims small and stable.

## What a bad pattern can suggest

- **Lambda consistently rich with negative fuel trims** → the DME pulling fuel to compensate; suspect leaking injectors or sensor error.
- Rich on one cylinder/bank → injector or sensor localized to that bank.
- Rich plus fouled plugs / fuel smell → confirm fueling source.

## Mechanical checks first

1. Inspect / test **injectors** for leakage; verify **coding**.
2. Check **O2 / wideband** sensor health.
3. Rule out mechanical causes affecting combustion.

## Tuning-related causes second

If hardware and sensors are confirmed good, a too-rich calibration can be adjusted — but only after ruling out leaking injectors and bad sensors. Do not lean out a tune to mask a leaking injector; fix the injector.

## When to stop tuning

Rich is less urgent than lean, but persistent rich running with fouling means stop and find the fueling/sensor fault before tuning further.
