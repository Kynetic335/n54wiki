# E30 / E50 Blend Calculator

Mixing your own ethanol blend is common on N54 builds — but getting the math right matters. Too little E85 and you're not seeing the charge-cooling benefit; too much and you're running a map that wasn't calibrated for your actual blend.

This calculator tells you exactly how many gallons of E85 to pump based on your current fuel level, your E85 pump's ethanol content, and your target blend.

## How the Math Works

The calculator solves for **gallons of E85 to add** (`x`) using a simple mixture equation:

```
x = current_fuel × (target% − current%) ÷ (E85_source% − target%)
```

**Example:** 7 gallons of E10 in a 15.9-gallon tank, targeting E30, using a 75% E85 pump:

```
x = 7 × (30 − 10) ÷ (75 − 30) = 7 × 20 ÷ 45 ≈ 3.11 gallons
```

Add 3.11 gal of E85, top off with 5.79 gal of 93 octane, done.

## Assumptions and Variables

### E85 Pump Ethanol Content

Pump E85 varies significantly — the ASTM standard allows 51–83% ethanol, and seasonal blending (more ethanol in summer, less in winter) means the same pump can swing 10–15 points between fills.

**The default of 75% is a reasonable mid-range estimate**, but if you want precision:
- Use an **ethanol content tester** (inline or handheld) to measure your actual blend after filling
- Many tuners require a measured baseline before the first WOT pull on a new batch

### Current Ethanol % in Your Tank

If you're on E10 pump gas, use **10%**. If you're filling from empty, use **10%** (residual fuel). If you already have an ethanol blend in the tank from a previous fill, measure it with a tester and enter that number.

### Tank Capacity

Common N54 tank sizes:
- **E90/91/92/93 335i, 135i** — 15.9 gal
- **E82/88 128i** — 14.9 gal
- **Z4 E89 35i** — 15.6 gal
- **F30 335i** — 15.6 gal

## Before Your First WOT Pull

1. **Verify your blend.** An ethanol tester costs $20–40 and removes all guesswork. Don't rely solely on the calculator if your E85 pump content is unknown.
2. **Have the right tune.** E30 and E50 require maps calibrated for those blends — running an E30 tune on E10 or vice versa will cause timing issues. See [Fuel Grades & Tune Compatibility](/fueling).
3. **Check your fuel system.** E50+ demands a healthy LPFP. See [HPFP / LPFP / Port Injection](/fueling/hpfp-lpfp-port-injection).
4. **Log your first WOT pull.** Monitor timing corrections, lambda, and fuel trims after any blend change. See [AFR / Lambda / Fuel Trims](/datalogging/afr-lambda-fuel-trims).
