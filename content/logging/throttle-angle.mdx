# Logging Throttle Angle

> **Source confidence:** High confidence (concept). Limiter behavior — verify by VIN/ROM ID.

## What the channel means

Throttle angle is how far the DME has actually opened the throttle plate. The N54 is torque-controlled: the **pedal** requests torque, and the DME decides the **throttle angle** needed to deliver it. So throttle angle is best read **next to pedal position** — the difference between "what the driver asked" and "what the DME allowed" is where the diagnosis lives.

## Why it matters

When throttle angle drops while the pedal is still flat, the DME chose to make less torque — a **throttle closure**. That is the fingerprint of a protective intervention or a limiter, and it explains a whole class of "the car cut / hesitated" complaints.

## What normal behavior generally looks like

- During a WOT pull, throttle angle goes to (near) full and **stays there** while the pedal is flat.
- Throttle tracks pedal smoothly in part-throttle driving.

## What abnormal behavior can suggest

- **Throttle angle dropping with pedal at 100%** → a closure. Then check *why*:
  - Lines up with **knock / high IAT / rail-pressure drop** → protection; fix that cause.
  - Happens at a repeatable **torque/load ceiling** with no protective trigger → a calibration **limiter** or drivetrain cap.
- Throttle not matching pedal in normal driving → possible throttle body or pedal sensor fault.

## Common interpretation mistakes

- Reading throttle angle **without pedal position** — you cannot tell a closure from normal lift.
- Assuming every closure is a "tune limiter" without checking protective triggers first.
- Removing a limiter to stop a closure that was actually protection (knock/heat/fuel).
- Ignoring sensor faults as a cause of odd throttle behavior.
