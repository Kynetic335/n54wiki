# Timing Corrections

> **Source confidence:** Needs validation

## What it is

Timing corrections are the advance the DME removes (and sometimes adds back) in response to knock detection. Negative corrections mean the DME is pulling timing.

## Why it matters

Timing corrections are one of the most important safety signals in a log. Persistent pull indicates knock from fuel, heat, or mechanical issues.

## How it works

Knock sensors detect abnormal combustion; the DME retards timing on affected cylinders to protect the engine.

## Common symptoms or failure modes

- Negative timing corrections under load
- Power loss
- Possible audible knock

## What to check in logs

- Timing corrections by cylinder
- IAT
- Fuel/lambda for context

## Mechanical checks first

Check fuel grade/blend, plugs, coils, and IAT before assuming the tune is wrong.
