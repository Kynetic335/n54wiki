# LPFP Failure

> **Source confidence:** High confidence (behavior). Pressure values — verify by VIN/ROM ID.

## What the symptom means

The LPFP (low-pressure fuel pump) is the in-tank pump that feeds the HPFP. When it weakens or fails, the low side cannot supply enough fuel, so the HPFP gets starved and high-side rail pressure falls under load. On higher-power and **high-ethanol** builds — which demand much more fuel volume — the LPFP is often the first part to run out, even when the HPFP is fine.

## Common causes

- **Worn / failing in-tank LPFP.**
- **Undersized stock LPFP** for the power/ethanol level (a capacity limit, not a defect).
- Wiring / connector / fuel-level sender issues affecting supply.
- Fuel contamination / clogged strainer.

## What to log

- [LPFP pressure](/wiki/logging/lpfp-pressure) — under load
- [HPFP / rail pressure](/wiki/logging/hpfp-pressure) — to see the downstream effect
- [Lambda / AFR](/wiki/logging/lambda-afr)

## What a healthy pattern looks like

- Low-side pressure **holds steady** through the pull.
- Rail pressure tracks target.
- Lambda at target.

## What a bad pattern can suggest

- **Low-side pressure sagging under load**, then **rail pressure crashing** → LPFP is the root limit ([Fuel Pressure Crash](/wiki/diagnostics/fuel-pressure-crash)).
- Sag worse after a **high-ethanol fill** → capacity limit for that blend.
- Erratic low-side pressure → failing pump or wiring.

_Media placeholder: LPFP-sag-then-rail-crash log screenshot._

## Mechanical checks first

1. Test **low-side supply pressure** under demand.
2. Check **wiring / connector** and pump current draw if possible.
3. Confirm **actual ethanol blend** vs what the system was built for.
4. Upgrade/replace the LPFP if it cannot keep up.

## Tuning-related causes second

The tune exposes a low-side limit by demanding more fuel; it does not cause the pump to fail. The fix is more low-side capacity (upgraded LPFP), not reducing targets to mask the sag.

## When to stop tuning

**Stop** if low-side sag leads to a rail-pressure crash and lean lambda under boost. Upgrade the fuel supply before further load.
