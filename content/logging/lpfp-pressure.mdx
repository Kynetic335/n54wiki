# Logging LPFP Pressure

> **Source confidence:** High confidence (concept). Pressure values — verify by VIN/ROM ID.

## What the channel means

LPFP pressure is the **low-side supply pressure** from the in-tank low-pressure pump that feeds the high-pressure pump (HPFP). It is the bottom of the fuel chain. The HPFP can only pressurize what the LPFP delivers, so low-side health sets the ceiling for the whole fuel system.

## Why it matters

On stock-ish cars the LPFP is rarely the limit. On **higher-power builds and high-ethanol blends** — which need much more fuel volume — the LPFP often runs out **before** the HPFP. When the low side sags, the HPFP gets starved and high-side rail pressure crashes. Logging LPFP pressure is how you tell whether the limit is the low side or the high side.

## What normal behavior generally looks like

- Low-side pressure **holds steady** through the pull.
- No sag as fuel demand peaks at high rpm/load.

## What abnormal behavior can suggest

- **Low-side pressure dropping under sustained load** → LPFP at its limit; it is starving the HPFP. See [LPFP Failure](/wiki/diagnostics/lpfp-failure).
- LPFP sag occurring **just before** the high-side [rail pressure crash](/wiki/diagnostics/fuel-pressure-crash) → confirms the low side is the root limit.
- Erratic low-side pressure → wiring/connector or failing pump.

## Common interpretation mistakes

- Looking only at **rail (high-side) pressure** and missing that the low side caused the crash.
- Blaming the HPFP when the LPFP was actually starving it.
- Forgetting that **higher ethanol = more volume** — a low side that was fine on pump gas may sag on E-blends.
- Treating a supply limit as a tuning value to dial around instead of a pump to upgrade.
