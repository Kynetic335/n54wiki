# Throttle Closures

> **Source confidence:** Needs validation

## What it is

Throttle closures are torque interventions where the DME closes the throttle to reduce output — for protection, limiting, or smoothing.

## Why it matters

Unexpected throttle closures during a pull are a key diagnostic clue: a limiter or fault is capping torque.

## How it works

When the torque model or a protection routine demands less torque, the DME closes the throttle even if the pedal is down.

## Common symptoms or failure modes

- Throttle angle dropping mid-pull while pedal is flat
- Power surges/cuts

## What to check in logs

- Throttle angle
- Pedal position
- Torque request
- Requested vs actual load

## Mechanical checks first

Distinguish protective closures (heat, knock, fueling) from limiter closures.
