# Logging Gear & RPM

> **Source confidence:** High confidence (concept). Specifics — verify by VIN/ROM ID.

## What the channels mean

Gear and RPM describe the conditions of a pull rather than the health of any one system. Gear tells you how much the engine was loaded (a higher gear loads it longer); RPM timestamps where in the rev range everything else happened. They are the backbone that makes every other channel interpretable.

## Why it matters

Logs are only comparable when taken under the same conditions. A standardized pull — commonly 3rd gear (see [Reading a 3rd Gear Pull](/wiki/logging/reading-a-3rd-gear-pull)) — in a consistent rpm window lets you compare boost, fueling, and timing across runs and across changes. Without gear/rpm context, two logs cannot be fairly compared.

## What normal behavior generally looks like

- A steady WOT pull stays in **one gear** through the rev range.
- RPM climbs smoothly to the target without unexpected drops or limiter hits.
- Pulls are repeatable in the same gear/rpm window.

## What abnormal behavior can suggest

- **RPM dropping mid-pull** with no shift → a torque cut / [throttle closure](/wiki/diagnostics/throttle-closures) or limiter.
- A pull that **shifted gears** partway → not a valid comparison; redo it.
- Hitting a rev limit earlier/later than expected → check [rev-limiter](/wiki/maps/rev-limiters) calibration or a misfire-driven cut.

## Which diagnostics use this channel

- Effectively all — gear/rpm provide context for every diagnostic.
- Especially [Throttle Closures (Diagnosis)](/wiki/diagnostics/throttle-closures) and anything compared across pulls.

## Common interpretation mistakes

- Comparing pulls taken in **different gears** (different load, not comparable).
- Comparing different **rpm windows** or partial pulls.
- Ignoring an unintended **shift** in the middle of a logged pull.
- Treating a 1st/2nd-gear pull as equivalent to a 3rd-gear pull.
