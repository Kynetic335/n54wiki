# Boost Control / WGDC

Understanding how the N54 manages boost pressure — and how to read that data in your logs — is essential for diagnosing boost-related issues.

---

## How N54 Boost Control Works

The N54 uses **wastegate duty cycle (WGDC) solenoids** to control boost pressure. Here's the system flow:

1. Boost pressure builds as the turbos spool
2. The ECU compares actual boost to the target (setpoint)
3. A **boost control solenoid** on each turbo receives a PWM signal from the ECU
4. The solenoid regulates pressure to the wastegate actuator
5. Higher duty cycle = more pressure holds the wastegate closed = more boost
6. Lower duty cycle = wastegate opens = boost bleeds off

The N54 runs **two wastegate solenoids** — one for each turbo (front and rear). They are controlled independently.

---

## WGDC Log Channels

In MHD, the relevant channels are:

- `WGDC front (%)` — duty cycle for the front turbo wastegate solenoid
- `WGDC rear (%)` — duty cycle for the rear turbo wastegate solenoid
- `Charge air pressure actual` — actual boost reading
- `Charge air pressure setpoint` — ECU boost target

## Reading Boost Logs

### Healthy boost trace
A clean boost log shows:
- Rapid spool from part throttle
- Actual pressure closely tracks the setpoint (within ~50–100 mbar)
- Stable hold at peak boost through the pull
- Gradual decline near redline as exhaust energy drops

### Common problems visible in boost logs

#### Underboost (actual below setpoint)
Boost actual consistently below target. Possible causes:
- **Worn WGDC solenoids** — cannot hold sufficient pressure on wastegate actuator; WGDC climbs high trying to compensate
- **Boost leak** — check all charge pipe connections, BOV, and intercooler couplers
- **Failing wastegate actuator** — spring too weak, diaphragm torn
- **Boost map calibration** — if the tune is too aggressive for what the car can deliver

#### Overboost (actual above setpoint)
Boost exceeds target. Causes:
- **Stuck wastegate** — actuator not opening when commanded
- **Solenoid failure in closed position** — holds WG shut regardless of command
- **Atmospheric reference leak** — uncommon but possible

Overboost causes the ECU to enter overboost protection, often resulting in a power cut and fault codes (30B9, 2FBC series).

#### Boost oscillation
Actual boost bounces above and below setpoint in a wave pattern. Usually indicates:
- Worn or failing boost solenoid(s) — inconsistent PWM response
- PID control fighting an unstable system
- Boost leak that appears and disappears based on pressure level

## WGDC Solenoid Replacement

The stock N54 boost solenoids are a known wear item. WGDC values climbing higher over time (to achieve the same boost target) is a classic sign of solenoid wear.

### N63 Solenoid Upgrade
Many N54 owners replace the stock boost solenoids with units from the BMW N63 (V8 twin-turbo), which are available OEM and considered more robust. Confirm fitment with your specific N54 application.

### Replacement interval
There's no fixed interval, but if your car is showing inconsistent boost or high WGDC readings for a given boost target, inspect and consider replacing the solenoids.

---

## Typical WGDC Values

WGDC values depend on the tune, turbo condition, solenoid condition, and target boost level. No single number applies universally. What matters is:

- **Consistency**: WGDC should be stable at a given RPM and boost target
- **Headroom**: If WGDC is at or near 100% trying to hit target and still underboosing, the system cannot deliver that boost reliably
- **Symmetry**: Front and rear WGDC should be relatively similar. Large asymmetry may indicate a hardware issue on one side
