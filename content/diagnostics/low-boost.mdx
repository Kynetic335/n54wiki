# Low Boost

> **Source confidence:** High confidence (diagnostic approach). Specific values — verify by VIN/ROM ID.

## What the symptom means

Low boost (underboost) is when the engine does not reach the boost pressure the DME is requesting. It feels like the car is "flat" or sluggish, and it frequently triggers a boost-deviation fault such as [30FF](/wiki/diagnostics/30ff-underboost). Low boost is a symptom, not a diagnosis — the value being low is the clue, not the answer.

## Common causes

- **Charge-path leak** — cracked chargepipe, popped intercooler coupler, split end tank, bad clamp.
- **Boost-control vacuum leak** — cracked lines, failed check valve, leaking reservoir/canister.
- **Diverter valve failure** — leaking on lift / under load.
- **Wastegate wear / rattle** — cannot hold position on aging turbos.
- **Boost solenoid failure** — cannot actuate the wastegate correctly.
- **Worn or damaged turbos** — past their useful airflow ceiling.
- **Clogged or restrictive intake / dirty filter** — restricts airflow.
- **Tuning that asks for more than the hardware can deliver** (see below).

## What to log

- [Boost target vs actual](/wiki/logging/boost-target-vs-actual)
- [WGDC](/wiki/logging/wgdc)
- RPM, gear, IAT

## What a normal log pattern looks like

- Actual boost climbs and stays near target across the pull.
- WGDC is at a reasonable, non-maxed level.
- Spool is smooth and repeatable pull to pull.

## What a bad log pattern looks like

- Actual boost noticeably below target.
- **WGDC pinned near maximum while boost is still low** — strong sign of a leak or a wastegate that cannot hold. The controller is "asking for everything" and not getting it.
- Boost that builds then bleeds off (leak that opens under pressure).

## Mechanical checks first

1. **Smoke test** the entire charge path — highest-value first step.
2. Inspect the **chargepipe** and all couplers/clamps.
3. Inspect **vacuum lines** and boost-control vacuum parts.
4. Check **diverter valves**.
5. Check for **wastegate rattle** and arm play.
6. Inspect the **intake/filter** for restriction.

## Tuning-related causes second

After the car is proven leak-free and the boost hardware is healthy:

- The calibration may request boost the current turbos/fuel/heat cannot support.
- Boost-control (WGDC base/correction) calibration may not match the hardware.

Never lower the underboost detection threshold to make a code disappear. That hides a genuine shortfall.

## When to stop tuning

Stop and fix the car first if a smoke test finds a leak, if WGDC is maxed with low boost, or if boost falls off mid-pull. Tuning cannot seal a leak or rebuild a worn turbo.
