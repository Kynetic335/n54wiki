# HPFP (High-Pressure Fuel Pump)

> **Source confidence:** Needs validation

## What it is

The HPFP is the engine-driven high-pressure pump that raises fuel to the high rail pressure direct injection requires. It is a well-known N54 wear/failure item.

## Why it matters

HPFP capacity and reliability set a hard limit on fueling. A failing HPFP causes long crank, fuel pressure crashes, and limp mode.

## How it works

Driven off the engine, the HPFP pressurizes fuel to the rail; the DME targets a rail pressure and adjusts as demand changes.

## Common symptoms or failure modes

- Long crank
- Rail pressure crash under load
- Limp mode
- Rough running

## What to check in logs

- Fuel rail pressure target vs actual
- HPFP-related fault flags

## Mechanical checks first

Confirm rail pressure holds under load before tuning. A worn HPFP mimics tuning faults.

## Related codes or fault patterns

- Fuel pressure faults (e.g. 29DC) — verify exact code by scan.
