# Fuel Rail Pressure

> **Source confidence:** Needs validation

## What it is

Fuel rail pressure is the high-side pressure at the injectors. The DME targets a value and the HPFP works to maintain it.

## Why it matters

Rail pressure holding its target under load is a core health and safety signal. Dropping below target under load indicates a fueling limit.

## How it works

The DME commands a target rail pressure; sensors report actual. The gap between target and actual reveals pump/supply capacity.

## Common symptoms or failure modes

- Actual rail pressure falling below target at high load
- Power loss, fault codes

## What to check in logs

- Fuel rail pressure target vs actual
- LPFP pressure
- Load and timing for context

## Mechanical checks first

Verify pump health and supply before assuming the tune. Mechanical fuel-system wear mimics tuning problems.

## Related codes or fault patterns

- Fuel pressure faults (e.g. 29DC) — verify by scan.
