# Diverter Valves

> **Source confidence:** Needs validation

## What it is

Diverter valves (DVs) recirculate compressed air when the throttle closes, protecting the turbos from surge. The N54 came with valves prone to wear.

## Why it matters

Failed DVs cause boost leaks, hesitation, and loss of boost — often mistaken for a tuning fault.

## How it works

On throttle lift, the DV opens to vent compressor pressure back upstream, avoiding compressor surge.

## Common symptoms or failure modes

- Loss of boost
- Hesitation
- Stumble on throttle transitions

## What to check in logs

- Boost target vs actual

## Mechanical checks first

Inspect/replace worn diverter valves before tuning boost.
