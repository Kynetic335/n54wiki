# Load / Torque

The Load / Torque section explains the torque-based control model the N54 DME uses: engine load, requested vs actual load, the torque request, driver demand, and the limiters and throttle interventions that cap power. Understanding this model is key to reading logs and understanding why the car cuts power.

## Pages in this section

- **[What Load Means](/wiki/load-torque/load-meaning)** — Engine load defined.
- **[Requested Load](/wiki/load-torque/requested-load)** — The load the DME is asking for.
- **[Actual Load](/wiki/load-torque/actual-load)** — The load the engine actually achieves.
- **[Torque Request](/wiki/load-torque/torque-request)** — The torque-based control core.
- **[Driver Demand](/wiki/load-torque/driver-demand)** — Pedal-to-torque mapping.
- **[Throttle Closures](/wiki/load-torque/throttle-closures)** — When the DME closes the throttle to cut torque.
- **[Torque Limiters](/wiki/load-torque/torque-limiters)** — Caps on requested/delivered torque.
- **[Load Ceiling](/wiki/load-torque/load-ceiling)** — The maximum load the DME allows.
- **[Load to Torque](/wiki/load-torque/load-to-torque)** — How load relates to torque output.
