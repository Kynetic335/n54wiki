# Checksums

> **Source confidence:** Needs validation

## What it is

Checksums are integrity values stored in the ROM so the DME can verify the calibration has not been corrupted. This wiki explains the concept; it does not provide checksum-correction steps.

## Why it matters

Checksums are part of why ROM matching and careful handling matter. A mismatched or corrupted image can fail validation.

## How it works

The DME computes a value over regions of the ROM and compares it to stored values. Mismatch indicates corruption or modification.
