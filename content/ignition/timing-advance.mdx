# Timing Advance

> **Source confidence:** Needs validation

## What it is

Timing advance is how far before top dead center the spark fires. More advance can make more power up to the knock limit; too much causes knock.

## Why it matters

Advance is balanced against knock. The DME targets advance and pulls it back when knock is detected.

## How it works

The base timing maps set target advance; corrections subtract advance when knock sensors detect activity.

## Common symptoms or failure modes

- Knock/timing pull under load
- Power loss when timing is corrected away

## What to check in logs

- Timing corrections (pull)
- Knock activity if available

## Mechanical checks first

Rule out fuel quality, plugs, and heat before changing timing.
