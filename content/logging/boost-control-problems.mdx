# Logging Boost Control Problems

> **Source confidence:** High confidence (concept). Specific values — verify by VIN/ROM ID.

## What the channel/topic means

Boost-control problems are read from two channels together: [boost target vs actual](/wiki/logging/boost-target-vs-actual) and [WGDC](/wiki/logging/wgdc). Boost control is "healthy" when actual tracks target with sensible WGDC. It is "in trouble" when actual misses target, hunts around it, or when WGDC behaves in a way the boost result does not justify. This page ties the boost diagnostics together.

## Why it matters

These two channels are how you separate a **mechanical** boost fault (leak, vacuum, wastegate, solenoid) from a **tuning** one. The classic tell: WGDC pinned high while boost stays low means the controller is doing everything it can and the air is escaping — a mechanical problem, not a map to change.

## What normal behavior generally looks like

- Actual boost converges to target and holds.
- WGDC modulates sensibly, not pinned at max to maintain boost.
- Repeatable pull to pull.

## What abnormal behavior can suggest

- **Boost short + WGDC maxed** → leak, worn wastegate, or worn turbo. See [Low Boost](/wiki/diagnostics/low-boost), [30FF](/wiki/diagnostics/30ff-underboost).
- **Boost over target** → stuck wastegate / solenoid. See [Overboost](/wiki/diagnostics/overboost).
- **Boost hunting + WGDC swinging** → oscillation: vacuum/solenoid/wastegate or over-aggressive control. See [Boost Oscillation](/wiki/diagnostics/boost-oscillation).
- Boost result that does not match WGDC at all → sensor or control fault.

## Common interpretation mistakes

- Reading boost **without WGDC** (or WGDC without boost) — you lose the diagnosis.
- "Raise WGDC in the tune" when the controller is already maxed against a leak.
- Softening boost-control tuning to hide a vacuum/wastegate fault.
- Comparing boost control across different gears/temps/tunes.
