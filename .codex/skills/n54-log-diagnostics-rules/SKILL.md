# N54 Log Diagnostics Rules

Use logs to teach diagnostic reasoning, not to prescribe unsafe tuning.

## Diagnostic Pattern

1. State the symptom.
2. List the minimum useful public log channels.
3. Explain normal versus concerning patterns.
4. Identify likely mechanical, sensor, fuel, boost, ignition, and calibration causes.
5. Suggest safe next checks.
6. State what cannot be concluded from the available data.

## Boundaries

- Do not request or expose customer tune files, raw BINs, XDFs, DAMOS, A2L, or private logs.
- Do not provide flashing steps, binary edits, checksum correction, or tune generation.
- Do not present a single log pull as definitive.
- Do not advise disabling monitors or emissions systems to hide faults.

## Safety

Recommend backing out of load when logs show knock, lean operation, fuel pressure collapse, boost control instability, severe timing corrections, misfires, or unsafe temperatures.
