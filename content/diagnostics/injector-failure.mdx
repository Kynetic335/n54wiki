# Injector Failure

> **Source confidence:** High confidence (behavior). Specifics — verify by VIN/ROM ID.

## What the symptom means

The N54's direct injectors spray fuel into each cylinder at high pressure. A failing injector — clogged, leaking, electrically weak, or simply **mis-coded** after a swap — delivers the wrong amount or pattern of fuel for its cylinder. That shows up as misfires, rough running, uneven fuel trims, or long crank, and is easy to confuse with ignition or tuning faults.

## Common causes

- **Clogging / fouling** restricting flow.
- **Leaking injector** (over-fuels its cylinder → rich/fouling).
- **Electrical / mechanical failure** of the injector.
- **Wrong or missing injector coding** after replacement (very common, fully preventable).

## What to log

- Misfire counters **per cylinder**
- [STFT / LTFT](/wiki/logging/stft-ltft) (and per-bank if available)
- [Lambda / AFR](/wiki/logging/lambda-afr)
- [HPFP / rail pressure](/wiki/logging/hpfp-pressure) for context

## What a healthy pattern looks like

- Misfire counters near zero.
- Fuel trims small and even across banks.
- Lambda at target.

## What a bad pattern can suggest

- **One cylinder misfiring** with otherwise good ignition → that injector.
- **One bank rich/lean** vs the other → injector or sensor on that bank.
- Rough running right after an **injector swap** → likely wrong/missing coding.
- Long crank from a leaking injector bleeding pressure down.

## Mechanical checks first

1. **Swap-test** / inspect the suspect injector.
2. Confirm **injector coding** matches the physically installed injectors.
3. Check **rail pressure** to rule out a system-wide fuel-supply issue.
4. Inspect plugs/coils to separate ignition misfires from fueling misfires.

## Tuning-related causes second

Injector problems are hardware/coding, not a tuning value. Do not add or pull fuel in the calibration to mask a leaking or mis-coded injector — correct the injector and its coding.

## When to stop tuning

If a cylinder is misfiring or a bank is off due to an injector, fix it before tuning. Uneven fueling makes every other reading unreliable.
