# 30FF Underboost

> **Source confidence:** High confidence (symptom behavior). Exact code text — verify by scan.

## What the code means

30FF is one of the most commonly reported N54 fault codes. In plain terms, the DME asked for a certain amount of boost (its target) and the engine did not reach it (actual fell short by more than the allowed margin), so the DME logged a boost-deviation / underboost fault.

30FF is a **result code**, not a root cause. It tells you boost came up short. It does not tell you *why*. The job is to find the cause — and on the N54 the cause is mechanical far more often than it is tuning.

The exact wording and threshold of the code is DME/ROM specific. **Verify by scan / Verify by ROM ID.**

## Common causes

Ordered roughly most-to-least common in the real world:

- **Boost leak** — cracked or popped charge pipe, loose intercooler coupler, split intercooler end tank, bad clamp.
- **Vacuum leak in the boost-control system** — cracked vacuum lines, failed check valve, leaking vacuum reservoir/canister.
- **Failed or sticking diverter valves (DVs)** — leak past the compressor on lift/under load.
- **Failing wastegate / wastegate rattle** — worn wastegate linkage on aging turbos, can no longer hold position.
- **Failing boost-control solenoids** — cannot route vacuum to position the wastegates correctly.
- **Worn turbos** — past their airflow ceiling, cannot make the requested boost.
- **Tuning that requests more boost than the hardware/condition can deliver** — see "Tuning-related causes" below.

## What to log

Log a full pull (see [Reading a 3rd Gear Pull](/wiki/logging/reading-a-3rd-gear-pull)) with at least:

- [Boost target vs actual](/wiki/logging/boost-target-vs-actual)
- [WGDC (wastegate duty cycle)](/wiki/logging/wgdc)
- RPM and gear
- IAT (to rule out heat)

## What a normal log pattern looks like

- Actual boost rises and tracks close to target through the pull.
- WGDC sits at a sensible value and is **not pinned at its maximum** to hold boost.
- No sudden drop in actual boost partway through the pull.

## What a bad log pattern looks like

- **Actual boost well below target**, especially building partway up then falling away.
- **WGDC pinned high (near max)** while boost still will not reach target — classic sign the DME is trying everything and the air is escaping or the wastegate cannot hold. This points hard at a **mechanical leak or wastegate problem**, not the tune.
- A sharp boost drop at a specific point can indicate a coupler letting go under pressure.

## Mechanical checks first

1. **Smoke test the charge path** — the single most useful test for 30FF. Find leaks at the chargepipe, couplers, intercooler, and intake.
2. Inspect the **chargepipe** (the plastic factory pipe is a known crack point).
3. Inspect **vacuum lines** and the boost-control vacuum components for cracks/disconnects.
4. Check **diverter valves** for wear/leaking.
5. Listen for **wastegate rattle**; check wastegate arm play on aging turbos.

## Tuning-related causes second

Only after the car is mechanically sound:

- A calibration that targets more boost than the current turbos/fuel/heat conditions can support can throw 30FF even with no leak.
- Boost-control (WGDC) calibration that does not match the hardware can under-deliver.

Do **not** "fix" 30FF by lowering the fault threshold or masking the code. That hides a real shortfall and can let a leak or failing turbo go unaddressed.

## When to stop tuning

Stop and fix hardware first if:

- WGDC is pinned high and boost still misses target.
- A smoke test finds any leak.
- Boost drops suddenly mid-pull.

Do not keep pulling or chasing the number in software until the mechanical side is proven healthy.
