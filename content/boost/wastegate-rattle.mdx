# Wastegate Rattle

> **Source confidence:** Needs validation

## What it is

Wastegate rattle is a rattling noise (often at light throttle/idle off-boost) caused by worn wastegate arms/flappers on aging turbos.

## Why it matters

It signals turbo wear and can correlate with boost control problems and a falling boost ceiling.

## How it works

Play in the wastegate linkage lets parts rattle when not under boost pressure.

## Common symptoms or failure modes

- Rattle at low throttle
- Boost control inconsistency

## What to check in logs

- Boost target vs actual
- WGDC

## Mechanical checks first

Confirm rattle source; severe rattle indicates turbo replacement/rebuild, not a tune fix.
