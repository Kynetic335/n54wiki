# Logging Timing Corrections

> **Source confidence:** High confidence (concept). Acceptable amounts — verify by VIN/ROM ID.

## What the channel means

Timing-correction channels show the ignition advance the DME **removes** (negative) when its knock detection hears abnormal combustion, and sometimes restores once conditions improve. On the N54 these are typically available **per cylinder**, which makes them powerful for isolating problems.

## Why it matters

Knock is destructive. Timing corrections are the engine's own defense, and watching them is how you confirm a pull is safe. Sustained or growing negative corrections mean the engine is knocking and the tune/fuel/heat/hardware combination is at or past its limit.

## What normal behavior generally looks like

- Little to no timing pulled under load.
- Any small corrections are brief and recover.
- All cylinders behave **similarly**.

## What abnormal behavior can suggest

- **Sustained negative corrections** under load → knock; check fuel grade, ethanol content, heat, plugs.
- **Corrections growing with [IAT](/wiki/logging/iat)** → heat-related (heat soak, weak intercooling).
- **One cylinder pulling much more than the rest** → cylinder-specific hardware (plug, coil, injector), not the whole tune.
- Timing pull alongside **lean [lambda](/wiki/logging/lambda-afr) or a [rail-pressure crash](/wiki/logging/hpfp-pressure)** → fuel-supply-driven knock.

## Common interpretation mistakes

- Panicking over a single tiny, brief correction vs a repeatable trend.
- Reading total/averaged timing without checking **per-cylinder** — you miss a single bad cylinder.
- Ignoring **IAT and fuel** context, then blaming the tune for heat- or fuel-driven knock.
- "Fixing" knock by pulling timing in the tune on a car with bad plugs or bad fuel — that hides the real fault.
