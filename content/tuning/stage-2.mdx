# Stage 2

Stage 2 is the most popular tuning tier for the N54. It combines **a catless or high-flow catted downpipe, a front-mount intercooler (FMIC), and optionally an intake** with a custom ECU tune to extract significantly more power from the stock turbos.

---

## What Stage 2 Adds Over Stage 1

| Change | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Downpipe (catless or HFC) | Reduces exhaust backpressure; allows turbos to spool faster and higher |
| Front-mount intercooler (FMIC) | Drops charge air temps dramatically; protects against heat soak and knock |
| Intake | Improves airflow to the turbos; minor contribution but adds to overall package |
| Custom tune | Calibrated specifically for your hardware, fuel, and boost targets |

---

## Hardware Requirements

| Component | Stage 2 Requirement |
|---|---|
| Downpipe | Catless (maximum flow) or high-flow catted |
| Intercooler | **FMIC required** — TMIC becomes a bottleneck at higher power |
| Charge pipes | Aluminum — required at Stage 2 boost levels |
| Intake | Aftermarket recommended |
| Spark plugs | Iridium, gapped 0.018–0.022" (tighter at higher boost) |
| HPFP | Must be healthy; replace if suspect |
| Boost solenoids | Inspect and replace if worn — critical for stable boost at Stage 2 |

### On the Downpipe
A catless downpipe will throw CEL codes for rear O2 sensors. A software delete (O2 simulator) or hardware bung cap handles this. A high-flow catted (HFC) downpipe eliminates the CEL at a slight power cost.

---

## Expected Power Range (93 Octane)

General community ranges for a properly tuned Stage 2 build on 93 octane:

- Wheel horsepower: approximately 380–430 whp (varies significantly by tune and hardware)
- Wheel torque: approximately 380–440 wtq

On E30–E40 blends: additional power headroom. See [Fueling](/fueling) for ethanol guidance.

> These are **general community ranges** based on commonly shared dyno results. Your car's output depends on the quality of the tune, ambient conditions, fuel, and hardware choices.

---

## Intercooler Selection

The FMIC is the most impactful single hardware change for Stage 2. Key considerations:

- **Size:** Larger cores provide better cooling but may require longer charge pipe runs
- **Common choices:** Wagner, Mishimoto, Active Autowerke, Chargepipe.de — all have community validation
- **Charge pipes:** Most FMIC kits include charge pipe replacements; verify fitment and pipe material (aluminum preferred)

A good FMIC should keep IAT (Intake Air Temperature) within a reasonable range of ambient even on repeated pulls. Log IAT before and after FMIC installation to quantify the improvement.

---

## Critical Log Channels for Stage 2

| Channel | What to watch |
|---|---|
| Timing corrections | Must be near 0° — any consistent pull means the tune needs adjustment |
| HPFP pressure | Should track commanded rail pressure through full WOT |
| Boost actual vs. target | Should match target; oscillation means solenoid or wastegate issue |
| IAT | Should stay reasonable (well under 120°F in most conditions with a good FMIC) |
| Lambda / AFR | Should be slightly rich at WOT; lean spikes are a concern |
